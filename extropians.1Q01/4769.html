> "Nanotechnology R & D in the Asia Pacific Region"
>
> by
>
> Dr. Lerwen Liu, Technology Consultant
> Asian Technology Information Program (ATIP),
> Tokyo, Japan
>
> When: March 8, 11:30 - 1:30 PM
> Where: Applied Physics Building, Room 299
> Stanford University Campus
> Lunch will be provided
> *R.S.V. P. required*
> reply by March 6 to Yurika.Peterman@stanford.edu
>
> Description of talk:
>
> Dr Liu is a full-time Technology Consultant at ATIP based in Tokyo investigating Nanotechnology R & D in Japan and the
> rest of Asia Pacific region. Dr Liu has been actively assisting Asian governments in their nanotechnology R & D strategy
> planning. She will outline the most current nanotechnology research and development programs in both government and
> industry in the Asia Pacific countries. In particular the most recent Japanese nanotechnology initiative planning will be
> introduced. Dr Liu will also address the importance of national, regional and global coordination in nanotechnology
> development.
>
> Nanotechnology includes Nanoelectronics, Nanomaterials and Bio-nanotechnology.
> Nanotechnology funding policy/global comparisons will be discussed.
>
> Bio:
>
> Dr. Lerwen Liu is an Australian citizen of Chinese origin with a PhD in
> Physics from the University of New South Wales. She has done postdoctoral
> studies at the University of Tokyo and Scuola Normale Superiore, Pisa,
> Italy. Her research area is condensed matter physics focusing on low
> dimensional electronic systems.

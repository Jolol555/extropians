Both Science (which describes the results from the private company
Celera) and Nature (which has the data from the Human Genome Project)
have taken the unprecedented steps of making their entire issues
available for free on the web, at: http://www.nature.com/genomics/human/
and http://www.sciencemag.org/genome2001/. The Nature version seems
somewhat more web-friendly: they've split the main article into five
parts for easy display, and they have some educational materials which
are more accessible. But the full reports by both groups are present,
as well as all the initial analysis.

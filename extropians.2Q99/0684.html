Interest rates are set by supply and demand. If almost everyone were trying to lend substantial amounts, and almost no one creditworthy was willing to borrow similar amounts, real interest rates for sound loans would be extraordinarily low compared to what we are used to. Likewise for return on capital invested in stocks.

In the economic world, immortality already exists in a sense. Corporations, banks, foundations, endowments, perpetual trusts, governments and other such entities are potentially immortal. Some few of those entities which have been in operation for a century or more have compounded their gains into enormous wealth. (Example: DuPont, GE) But that was in an environment where safe real returns on capital were in the range of perhaps 4% to 8% per year.

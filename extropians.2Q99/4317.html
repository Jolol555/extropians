Except that Searle comes to exactly the OPPOSITE conclusion. He says that there is no consciousness in this case. He explicitly denies that if a result is computed by parts, then consciousness is a distributed property of those parts.
That is his whole point - that is why he is so bitterly against functionalists like Dennett.

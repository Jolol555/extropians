Every day that this undeclared war continues, it becomes more likely that ground troops will be deployed, and that we will find ourselves in another Vietnam-style quagmire. The bombing of Yugoslavia is a textbook example of the folly of foreign intervention: So far, the US-NATO air assault has accomplished the exact opposite of what President Clinton promised.

Bombing errors have resulted in the deaths of refugees -- the very people we are supposed to be protecting.

The job of the American military is to defend the United States and to keep Americans safe. Instead, our air assault on Yugoslavia has reduced our military preparedness, reignited Cold War tensions with Russia, and made us a more tempting target for terrorists.

If the American people don't speak out, the war will continue to escalate, ground troops will be eventually be deployed, and American casualties will rise. If we don't want that to happen, we must persuade our representatives in Congress to stop the war now.

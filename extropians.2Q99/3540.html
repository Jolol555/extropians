As a nutrition researcher and a vegetarian, I am convinced that the value of vegetarianism is high-fiber, low-fat, and other qualities that have little to do with animal-origin, and more to do with the fact that meat is fatty and low-fiber. Eating lean chicken or fish, and occasional lean beef can be good. Avoiding animal product totally can solve these problem easily, but then you have other issues in making sure you get enough protein and vitamin B12.

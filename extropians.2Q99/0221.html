Yes, I suppose so. But the justice system as we have it allows for false arrest and malicious prosecution redress only in very limited circumstances. It almost never happens that a suspect who is acquitted at trial goes back and successfully sues or prosecutes those who accused, arrested, and tried him. O.J. has not. It seems as if 'honest mistakes' by the system are allowed to go unpunished. One libertarian has proposed that in death penalty cases the jury vote on the death penaly for the convicted. If, after the convict is executed, he is later proven not guilty, then those jurors who voted for death would themselves be executed. I guess the death penalty would be very rarely applied under that system.

>Cyberpsychology explores de relationships between technology and the
>individual. Tracing historical and contemporary lines of argument, the
>text brings together psychologists and cultural theorists working in the
>spheres of technology and subjectivity to explore links between popular
>culture, technoscience, feminism, queer theory, ethics and politics.
>Wide-ranging and provocative, each chapter engages with mainstream
>psychological research and critical social trend to explore issues such as
>the collapse of memory and creativity and the applications of virtual
>technologies to the lives of people with disabilities. It is essential
>reading for anyone interested in critiques of psychology and the
>developing communications media.
>

As you can see from my new .signature, I have now installed BeOS on my machine. I am also typing this message on a Dvorak keyboard. In the last election, I voted Libertarian where possible. I'm a Singularitarian, and thus by extension a transhumanist, a science-fiction fan, and a novelty-seeker. Coincidence? I think not. For some people, There Is A Better Way.

Let us consider: Is someone running MacOS or Linux or BeOS, even though 95% of the planet uses Windows, more likely to use the Dvorak keyboard, even though 95% of the planet uses QWERTY? Is someone who votes Libertarian more likely to be a transhumanist? I have no hard statistics on the subject, but I suspect that there is considerable overlap between the groups.

I'd like to list the following items for a Better Way Award. The Better Way Awards are given to Ways (1) that inspire fanatic devotion in their users and (2) that only a minority use - and, of course, are vastly Better than the alternatives.

BeOS: There Is A Better Operating System. Linux/OpenSource: There Is A Better Development Strategy. Dvorak: There Is A Better Keyboard. Libertarianism: There Is A Better Political Party. Design Patterns: There Is A Better Design Method. Science Fiction: There Is A Better Literature. Singularity/>H: There Is A Better Goal. Cryonics: There Is A Better Survival Strategy. Nanotechnology: There Is A Better Material World.

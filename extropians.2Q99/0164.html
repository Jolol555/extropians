The "Right to Die" or rather right to receive a euthanasia is already legal in some states. Euthanasia is only separated from murder, the taking of life without consent, by express or implied consent, express in the case of those that request it being of sound mind, and implied through such measures as a "Living Will." I think it should only be sanctioned after psychiatric evaluation.

In this country, the United States of America, we have a system of appellate courts. Kevorkian's trial and judgment leads to the next stage, where Kevorkian might file an appeal. Kevorkian and the case judged against him is not necessarily the best representative of euthanasia rights. This is an issue for the Supreme Court at some point.

I do not have any desire to die.

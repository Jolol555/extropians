I gather ultracold singularities are formed mostly by fusions of
singularities/singularities singularities/stars (if their temperature is
noticeably below 3 K, they can then only grow, not evaporate). (This is
most paradoxical, for the quality of the energy sink increases the more we
use it as such!)

It would seem sensible to couple cold singularities with fusion power, and
hot holes for energy sources (difficult to detect, for semistationary
microsingularities do not emit significantly more than a nuke). However,
how does one keep the emission spectrum in the optimal range, i.e. keeping
one's PV arrays from being cooked while maxing out on output? (Ignoring
strange matter and boojums just for the sake of argument). Dumping matter
down a microhole will only make it worse: beware of the accretion disk.
Can attenuation of the ultrahard gamma and xRay be an option (emission
maximum homeostasis stops being a problem)? How would a far-sense
signature of such a power plant look like?

On 2/26/02 7:58 AM, "Richard Steven Hack" <richardhack@pcmagic.net> wrote:
> As for
> explosives, I knew a guy in the Federal pen who built a couple of pipe
> bombs to blow up a beaver dam on his property. He made the mistake of
> letting someone else in on it - got about four years.

While I can sympathize with that guy, in most locales anyone with a photo ID
can purchase high explosives legally and very cheaply (probably cheaper than
his manufactured pipe bombs). Or he could have filled out a short form that
would have allowed him to legally manufacture the stuff. About two thirds
of the legal explosive consumption in the U.S. is private individuals
purchasing it for a wide range of personal applications, and there is far
less red tape than if you were to do something such as purchase a car or buy
a firearm. Most people just assume you can't buy it when in fact just about
anyone can buy a truckload of gel packs if they so desire (and there IS a
wide-range of ordinary applications for which explosives are indispensable).

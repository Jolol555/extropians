Robert J. Bradbury wrote,
> My current business plan is on how to make robust bio-nanotechnology
available
> to large numbers of molecular biologists within *this* decade. It has
> been reviewed by Eric Drexler, Robert Freitas, and several people with
> training in genetics and molecular biology. They have not found any
> fundamental flaws in it.

(This is Harvey Newstrom, not Louis Newstrom to whom you have been
speaking.)

I would be very interested in reviewing any business plan that will get me
some nanotechnology for my purposes within this decade. I will expend great
amounts of money toward this if it is really possible.

I make the same offer to anyone who think they can within this decade
achieve:
- nanotechnology that I can use for my purposes
- human-level AI that I can use for my purposes
- human consciousness uploading that I can use for my purposes
- substantial increases in life-extension that I can use for my purposes

I will even fly out and pay for someone's time to pitch these plans to me.
I can't promise I will agree with your ideas or spend my money. But I do
promise if I am really convinced it will work and benefit me, I will gladly
devote my time and money into making it happen.

> "Here's why...At the speed of light, it would take 2.3 seconds for a
> photon from the center of the Sun to reach the surface."

This is in a vacuum. Now, I can't see the stars on the opposite side of
the sun from here, so it's rather obvious that the sun isn't a vacuum.
Furthermore, being more or less solid/liquid/plasma matter, it is
unlikely that a photon created in the core would ever survive to the
surface. It's energy would be absorbed and reemitted by many billion
billions of atoms between the core and the surface.

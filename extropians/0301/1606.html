On Tue, Jan 28, 2003 at 11:17:15PM -0500, Nathanael Allison wrote:
>
> I was wandering if anyone could point me to some sources on acting
> intelligent. Acting in a manner that is generally considered intelligent
> will make it that much easier when dealing with the not so intelligent, no
> offense to anyone of course. Thanks, Nate

The classic thing to ask oneself is "what would <X> do?" where <X> is
someone really smart.

Another fun exercise is 'conversation with the superbrain': you and a
friend are discussing something, and one of you play the role of a
really powerful intelligence and the other asks you questions which
you answer with smart answers. After a while you switch places. It is
surprising how much smarter you can get just by pretending.

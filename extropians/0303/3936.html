    From: Amara Graps [mailto:amara@amara.com]
    > For the best source of information on psychoactive substances
    > I recommend PiHKAL: A Chemical Love Story, by Alexander and
    > Ann Shulgin, 1991.

    This is a fabulous book indeed, as is its sequel TiHKAL. I consider
    Shulgin a highly extropic individual. His commitment to exploration
    through personal experimentation is impressive, to say the least.

    At the same time, I must say that erowid.org has become the best
    source of information on psychoactive substances. It covers a far
    broader set of substances than Shulgin's books, and provides deeper
    information about each. In the entires for substances that Shulgin
    discovered, it links to the online copies of his books.

    In particular I think erowid is useful for the breadth of its
    individual reports on the subjective effects of compounds. Reading
    Shulgin's books you only get reports from him and his small group of
    experimenters. On erowid you can often get dozens of reports from a
    wide ranging set of individuals.

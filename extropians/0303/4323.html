    The problem is that "karma" is an oversimplification. Take a look at
    the slashdot karma system. It does have a cumulative number, but the
    rankings fall into about 7 categories, including, e.g., funny,
    insightful, troll, flamebait, etc., and in addition to displaying the
    cumulative score of a post, the predominant category choosen is
    displayed. (I believe that the poster's karma, however, is only
    modified by the single numeric score.)

    You could do approximately the same thing with just displaying several
    different links below each post (instead of only two). The SlashCode is
    a rather complex Perl script that probably would be too complicated for
    most places, but I'm sure that there are simpler ways to do things, at
    least if you don't need to handle their volume. (OTOH, I believe that
    the SlashCode is open source, so if you want to put up such a site, and
    can hack Perl, it might be a good choice.)

    While my sigfile specifically singles out pacifists, I would say that
    anybody who isn't taking personal action to fight tyranny is aiding and
    abetting it by doing nothing. Pacifism is more than just an opinion or
    an attitude, it is also a lack of action. In a spiritual sense, it is a
    sin of omission.

    ====Mike, from your statements above and below, I feel that your mind is a
    sin of omission. Seriously you need to give me some more evidence before I
    can side with you on this one. From my view you've made a few statements
    about pacifism that don't match my own definition or what I would take as
    the standard definition. If you are creating a specialized definition then
    that should be noted with some sort of asterisk in your "sigfile" and all
    other places where you make reference to the concept.
    How is pacifism a lack of action? It seems rather easy, at first glance, to
    find numerous and reliable contradictions of the statement - "Pacifism is
    equivalent to in-action" Is making a decision a form of in-action? Or more
    broadly, can anything in existence be said to exist in a complete state of
    inactivity? and on a human level could anyone say that another human mind
    (except for your own:)) be absolutely and totally inactive?
    Ghandi is widely seen to be a "pacifist". Would you claim that he was not a
    pacifist? (Maybe he exhibited some form of passive-aggressiveness that moved
    him from the pacifist camp to the instigator of violence camp?) If you do
    agree that he was a pacifist would you then claim that he was in-active?
    That Ghandi was aiding in tyranny?
    Maybe you mean some special sort of inaction... in that case you would want
    to make special note of it. In this case the asterisk would again serve you
    well to explain your own special definition of the concept "action".
    It may be fun to play with words but sometimes this play leads to war.
    Please let me know what your line of reasoning is here. I'm, afraid of, but
    curious to know the workings of a mind like yours.

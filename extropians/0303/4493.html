    [snip]

    > On the really practical level, we have to find better ways of
    > communicating. I think part of it is the professionalisation of
    > transhumanism. Instead of having it as a hobby, we should make it our
    > job. It is great earning money and influence from doing what you like.
    > So the issue for each of us is how to either integrate transhumanism
    > into current jobs, or how to create jobs for ourselves that promote
    > transhumanism. It can range from being an academic over think tanks to
    > physical training.

    Yes, very good. My sense is that the one of the logical first steps is
    professionalisation in transhumanist media: strides have been made there in
    the last year, but I think that it's still in building the tools and growing
    the reader base phases. We're way behind the web comic industry in technical
    and social sophistication, for example.

    RMS once said something to the extent of not worrying about the money; if
    you're dead set, hard working and into what you really want to do, the money
    will just happen in due course. (Someone else once told me that the time to
    start a company/venture is when you can absolutely no longer refrain from
    starting a company/venture).

    But yes, professionalisation. Well said.

    > --- "Dickey, Michael F"
    > <michael_f_dickey@groton.pfizer.com> wrote:
    > > A cheap, simple implant anyone could get. Ideally,
    > > it would be even better
    > > if such a device could power external electronic
    > > devices.

    > It'd produce miniscule amounts of power - but then,
    > many devices only need miniscule amounts of power.

    I recall a bit from a Niven story (I think called "Veil of Anarchy")
    about a fellow who had a digital watch implanted beneath his skin.

    Given such devices currently run on batteries with lives of 3 years or
    more, I'd want something that consumes a bit more power if it is going
    to help me lose weight.

    What I'd like is an implanted PDA in my brain with a HUD in one or both
    eyes, a GPS unit, and a cell phone and FM/XM radio receiver built into
    my jaw and skull. The antenna would be a meshed phased array between
    skull and skin. Having such a broad antenna should minimize EM risk.
    The whole setup is voice and blink/point of focus controlled.

    Total consumption should be around 5 watts.

    To detail how NOT to lose weight:
    My mother went under the knife two months ago to get her stomach
    stapled. The arthritis in her knees makes knee replacement a needed
    thing, but the docs don't want to do it at her age while she weighs
    over 250 lbs, so she opted for surgery (she swims and works out several
    times a week, for those thinking she is being lazy. A thyroid removal a
    decade ago is the primary reason for her slow metabolism.)

    Being an individual who has never healed very well, her recovery from
    this operation has been about as bad as it could be. Three days after
    the operation, it was found that an old adhesion had kinked the small
    intestine, causing fluid to back up and burst a suture at the stomach.
    Her whole abdominal cavity was therefore infected. She eventually lost
    2/3 of her colon and a yard of her small intestine due to loss of blood
    flow. She spent over a month in the ICU, all of it sedated and with her
    incision wide open due to swelling preventing it from being sewed shut.
    She has been in the Vascular Surgery ward under 24 hour nursing care
    for the last month and is concious, but with a tracheotomy, a tube
    through the nose and four into her abdomen for draining fluid while she
    continues to heal.

    I am frankly quite shocked that surgeons today are still relying on
    plain old sutures to close abdominal incisions.

    I am also pissed that the docs demanded she lose this weight to get her
    artificial knees, since men that weight get them all the time. The docs
    claim they would wear out in 15 years. I said so freaking what! By then
    they could clone her some new knees fer ghus sake.

    Lastly, I am perturbed at what is considered acceptable nursing care
    today. She is at Dartmouth-Hitchcock Medical Center, which is
    considered a top notch facility, and the neglect and bad attitude of
    many of the nurses has annoyed us to no end. I pity those who have to
    stay at lower quality institutions. As Doctor MacCoy would say, "This
    is barbaric!"

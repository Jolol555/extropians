    Search the archives (on http://www.extropy.org/bbs) by clicking on their name
    then having the form retrieve their posts.

    Skim what the suspected conservatroll wrote. Is most of it about mere
    current events and mere 20th century politics that has sod-all to do with
    technologically transcending the petty limitations holding back human
    potential? Have they posted precious little about favorite Extropian topics
    like computers, AI, singularity, cryonics, life-extension, nanotech, biotech,
    space travel, etc.? Have they, in fact, posted ANYTHING AT ALL about ANY topic
    you consider to be Extropic?

    No? Well, as Sinead put it "By their own words, they will be exposed."

    No matter how invitingly, temptingly, seductively WRONG posts by such
    individuals are, it's serving their goals to reply, because it contributes
    to taking the list further off topic. That's what trolls, by definition,
    specialize in doing.

    Now, if only there were an "Ignore" feature on the BBS site, that would make
    it easier to resist the temptation... maybe even a collaborative ignore, where
    you can see how many other people are ignoring a particular email address, so
    as to make the process of developing your own ignore list more efficient...
    that would be pretty neat.

    spike66 wrote:
    > CNN is claiming that the Pakistanis are closing in
    > on bin Ladin. If he is taken, does it give anyone
    > else here hope that his capture could somehow prevent
    > a war with Iraq?

    Unfortunately the lack of any good reason whatsoever for this
    invasion hasn't stopped it moving toward actuality so far so I
    don't see that this will. Certainly there is not a shred of
    real evidence that Iraq has anything to do with bin Ladin and
    company. So how would this slow down the drive toward this
    catastrophe?

    > Are those two things linked in any
    > way?

    Not in the least.

    > Is it too optimistic of me to keep hoping the
    > Iraqi military will mutiny, collectively realizing
    > the absurdity of attempting a defense by flesh and
    > blood against silicon and steel?

    I am an even worse optimist. I keep hoping the US government
    will realize this is a pointless atrocity and back away. I keep
    thinking that maybe they will actually listen to the people at
    home and abroad who are vehemently against this.

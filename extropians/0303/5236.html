    Gts opined:

    <<FOX gives me an adrenaline rush, sometimes to the point where I can't stand
    it anymore. I might have liked it back in the 80's, when I was a
    20-something hard-line Reaganite who thought he had just "seen the light"...>>

    I like FoxNews the best, and find that in the "Foreign Policy" realm;
    Fox/Republicans are spot-on. Spot-off is CNN, and typically the least
    pro-American of the "news channels." MsNBC is rather good, in that it seems
    incisive; biting in the way Fox is, but less personality-dependent. The
    regular news orgs in the US, ABC, NBC, CBS, I rarely view at all, anymore.

    Peter Jennings personal relationship with PLO mouthpiece Hanan Ashwari is
    comparatively well-known. CBS is reflects Din Hewett's and Dan Rather's
    impatience. NBC relects Tom Brokaw's mumble-ability. I switch to FoxNews-and
    MsNbc, and view online news sources like the Washington Post and Washington
    Times. For news analysis (tell me what to think!!!) I enjoy the National
    Review, Weekly Standard, occasionally, The New Republic and the like.

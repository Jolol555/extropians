    It seems clear to me that Extropic is definitely in the eye of the beholder.
    This is an individualist philosophy that we are refering to; the
    interpretation of individuals must be paramount. Post-modernists might
    mutter something under their breaths about "multiplicity of discourse"; I'd
    simply say that each person is free to interpret Extropianism as he/she sees
    fit.

    Of course, that doesn't stop that anyone from being a stupid plonker (in the
    eyes of other individuals). But extropy is not the negative of the
    thermodynamic concept of entropy, and thus universal; it is a personal
    philosophy. While I may think that extropians should interpret the
    principles in a particular way, and you may think the same thing (although
    our particular definitions of extropy may differ), that doesn't make it so
    for anyone else.

    There is a unifying factor, which is rationality; it's in the principles.
    Might I humbly propose that we all take a few minutes to have a look at the
    principles today, read them and think about them just for a few minutes, and
    reflect on their relationship to our own recent posts? Leave aside
    consideration of other people's posts, no matter how vile you might consider
    them; just consider your own recent posting, in the light of the principles,
    and your own, hopefully somewhat internally consistent, interpretation of
    their meaning.

    For my part, I don't think that I can usefully post anything related to the
    current war for quite a while. Somehow all discussions turn to irrational
    crap, and end up being far worse than useless (imo). I know it's an
    important issue, I really do, and I feel really strongly about it. But, as
    objectively as I can, I have determined that there is just no point to
    further postings in regard of it, at least for the time being.

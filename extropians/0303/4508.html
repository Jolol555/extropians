    In response to Mike and Alex, although some may think we are being
    somewhat extreme: This is similar to the protesters in the US who are
    have recently been sneaking onto Air Force bases to hinder/sabotage
    the US military effort. The USAF has now stated that anyone sneaking
    onto Air Force bases will be shot. (period)

    So any day now the news will undoubtedly be that someone trespassed
    onto a military base and was, actually, shot.

    Does this criminalize the US, the USAF, or the US government? Well,
    no. "US Citizen" is not a license to do whatever you damn well
    please, and does not condone immunities or for that matter super
    powers.

    Which brings me to the bulldozer. Any loss of life is a sad affair.
    But social protest must abide by higher laws, and I don't mean
    international law over local law, I mean the laws of physics. You
    shouldn't even cross the street without making eye contact with the
    person in the car on the stop line, nevermind jump/walk or otherwise
    locomote in front of bulldozers.

    I find myself in agreement with the harsh reality of illegal
    combatants, and wonder if that will carry over onto American soil
    with this whole Air Base fiasco.

    Bret Kulakovich

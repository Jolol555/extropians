    Artillo writes

    > "YEEE HAWWWW!!!! Well ship me over thar with muh
    > trusty ole shotgun and a 6 pack of Bud! Weesa
    > goin ta war, boys! Ima gonna shoot me some o
    > dem dar Eeerakees!" - Redneck Joe Blow

    :-) Yes, but, joking aside, it is interesting that fundamentally
    the same psychology does appear to be in operation! I was highly
    amused by CNN or Fox News captioning Bush's upcoming talk with
    the words "Bush gives Hussein 48 hours to get out of Baghdad".

    It sounded exactly like Wyatt Earp or Matt Dillon giving a desperado
    an hour to get out of town, "or else!". But should we really be
    surprised that the same patterns animate nations' behavior toward
    each other as animate individuals?

    Moreover, as much as I did appreciate that characterization above,
    it's a dumb mistake to think that Bush's policy objectives and
    deepest strategy is being drawn up by good ol' boys from Texas.
    His closest public advisors---Rumsfeld, Powell, and Rice---are
    hardly the same "type". Neither are his national security advisors.
    So attacks on his "cowboy mentality" are nothing more than sneering
    prejudice against stereotypes.

    I wonder if Powell had become President whether those getting great
    emotional satisfaction from trashing U.S. foreign policy would be
    making jokes about blacks? Think so? Or is that verboten, while
    Texans are fair game?

    Lee

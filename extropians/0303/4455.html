    This CNN article describes the handwringing by parents
    who have discovered that the porn filters they have on
    their kid's internet connections can be easily defeated:

    http://www.cnn.com/2003/TECH/internet/03/14/downloading.porn/index.html

    This gave me an idea for a fun and educational little
    game we could play. Divide a group of extropian
    contestants into two teams, the Ps and the Ks. The
    Ks write up an essay describing their first sexual
    experience, or if they haven't had one yet, a
    fictional idealized sexual experience. The Ps write
    up a list of words or phrases that will filter out
    the Ks essays, these being "porn".

    Mix the Ks essays with a number of texts that have no
    specifically sexual subject matter, such as news
    articles, extropian messages in the archives or
    essays on medical topics from the internet.

    Winner of the P team is the one who writes the filter
    which most effectively identifies the Ks porn while
    filtering the fewest non-porn articles. The winning
    K is the one who writes the most provocative "porn"
    that makes it thru the most filters.

    Actually anyone could compete on both teams, writing
    an essay and a filter. This sounds like a fun little
    exercise in creative writing, as well as surely
    producing some hilarious material.

    Who wants to play?

    spike

    Stirling Westrup wrote:
    > On 9 Mar 2003 at 9:53, spike66 wrote:
    >>
    >>I question the premise. The quiet majority in most
    >>countries might like America and western Europe...
    >
    > I hate to burst your bubble, but as a Canadian who occasionally travels
    > abroad, I am often mistaken for an American. When I carefully point out that
    > I am, in fact, Canadian, the receptions get much warmer and the glowering
    > faces go away...

    Canada is also a special case. Everyone loves Canadians.
    There were a large number of canucks where I went to
    college in Washington state. They were pretty much every
    last one of em agreeable chaps, good hearted souls.
    Head in the clouds, feet on the ground, as we used to
    say. I carefully avoided dating Canadian girls, because
    they had a lovable attitude, a most pleasant disposition.
    My reasoning was if I fell for any of them, one of us
    would end up living far from home.

    > Americans may not exactly be "Hated" around the world, but
    > there are many places where you are more tollerated than welcome...

    Currently Americans are a grave danger to all
    bystanders wherever they travel. There are a
    number of extremists who will murder 100 innocents
    in case there is even one American present, such
    as was the case in the Bali nightclub bombing.
    They managed to kill two Americans there, along
    with a couple hundred others. Collateral damage
    cannot be avoided, you understand. Such as these
    can never be appeased, so Americans are a danger
    to everyone abroad.

    I propose a passport that is not tied to any one
    piece of real estate, one that is recognized in
    any of the United Nations or even a superset of that.

    I have a vision. Every nation on this planet
    will eventually be so integrated that conditions
    will be nearly identical everywhere, with some of
    everything, a little like an extended American
    suburb, with government systems that eventually
    converge, becoming so indistinguishable that
    borders are meaningless, superfluous constructs.
    In that day, wars will cease for the same reasons
    that my neighborhood does not make war on the one
    10 streets over, because they are completely
    interchangeable.

    All the world's diversity can coexist on every
    artificially subdivided plot of ground we now
    know as nations. All governments will be completely
    secular and will protect the rights of the minority
    against the majority, and in some cases the rights
    of the majority against the murderous minority.
    Every human on the planet will be able to choose
    any culture or way of life she wants, without even
    having to move. Universal freedom of choice.

    spike

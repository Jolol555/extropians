    --- Dehede011@aol.com wrote:
    > In a message dated 3/24/2003 8:15:41 AM Central Standard Time,
    > mlorrey@yahoo.com writes: To date, I have met very few anarchists who
    > are pro-gun. Those who are tend to be the very most militant and
    > hard-core types who are the heart of the anarchist movement. They
    > are Vietnam vets who served
    > in A-Teams and they see the current movement as simply a non-violent
    > prelude to an outright socialist revolution.
    >
    > Mike,
    > Are you saying that the anarchist pose is a sham and that they
    > are really revolutionary socialists in disguise?

    Talking to these anarchists, they are generally the sort that do not
    recognise anarcho-capitalism or American Libertarianism as a legitimate
    form of anarchy. They claim that the only real anarchists are
    socialists (as we've discussed the issue here before, I won't detail
    this further). It is these sort who are the heart of both the
    anti-globalization and the luddite movements.

    This is to distinguish such from the Western diamondback anarchist,
    generally a vet or otherwise gun toting individual who is foursquare
    for capitalism and the free market. Such are usually ex-Patriots who
    became disillusioned by the whole Ruby Ridge/Waco/ATF series of events
    in the last decade, but not exclusively so, and generally opinions
    differ based on who is in office. Some are ardent isolationists to the
    point of being prototypical "armadillos" as Vernor Vinge described.
    There are a few such on this list and attending Extro conferences.

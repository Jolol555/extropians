    Hubert writes

    > This is the first official announcement of the Secretary
    > of PUKE (Professional Uplifting Killing Experience)
    >
    > Bring your personal throw up to the local PC!

    May I ask what is the purpose of bringing the taste of this
    forum to a new low? Have you completely abandoned even the
    pretence of decorum and civility here?

    > From today on everybody who feels like he has to
    > puke when watching Fuehrer George Troubleyou Bush
    > addressing the US army,

    What makes you think that you have some prerogative to
    play the Hitler-card? Does it never occur to you that
    name calling ought to have no place here? In three
    posts in a row, you have gone way out of your way to
    express your extreme emotional overreactions. I'm
    not at all sure just what to say...

    So I'll put out a more general question. How is it that
    a current member of the nation of Germany, who has a
    history on this list of having engaged in a number of
    abstract conversations, is so traumatized by what he's
    seeing or hearing that he's coming to behave as a
    self-anointed "secretary of PUKE"?

    Now were he directly involved (say he lived in Iraq, or
    had lost family members in terrorist bombings, or had
    known friends who died at the hands of the armed services
    of the United States), then I might understand. But as it
    is, I simply don't.

    So perhaps we should all ask ourselves: Just what would
    have to happen to us to force us off the edge the way it
    has Hubert?

    Would one have to work in a Hospital emergency room? Would
    one have to interview rape victims for a living? Would one
    have to suffer the actual deprivations of combat, or, perhaps
    worse, live as a civilian in a combat zone? For the life of
    me, I can't quite see how watching television would ever
    bring me to the crisis that Hubert is evidently experiencing.

    Just what manner of international incident, especially
    were my own nation not involved, or descriptions of world
    catastrophes consuming millions of lives would bring this
    out in me?

    I know that some here have expressed wishes of pity for him,
    but it's unclear how sincere these were. (For me, I'm not
    really sure that he himself is suffering that much, or even
    absolutely confident that he's as emotionally overwrought to
    the degree it appears.) However, I can advise him to stand
    back from that which may be distressing him so much; perhaps
    for a few days or weeks, and try to regain a little stability.

    Also, there might be other lists on which he could vent his
    emotions and coarse metaphors? Eh?

    --- Randy <cryofan@mylinuxisp.com> wrote:
    > THis is why our govt can almost do as it pleases; this is why they
    > no longer fear that their heads may roll for their corruption:

    > http://www.wired.com/wired/archive/11.03/view.html?pg=5

    DeLong makes some allusions to facts, but fails to reason them through
    because he ignores other facts. For example, he blames market economics
    for the failure to distribute food evenly to the worlds population, yet
    famines in the last century occured ONLY in countries where governments
    committed significant interference in market economies, if not outright
    central command control of their economies.

    Malnutrition in the US can be shown to occur in areas where governments
    exhibit significant tax burdens on the local economy (large cities) to
    support socialist patron/client relationships or else are used to
    enforce patron/client oligopolistic local economies (appalachia, for
    example), neither of which is a free market.

    Politician's corruption, and our toleration of it, is more a reflection
    of how evenly the benefits of that corruption are distributed. The more
    average people that feel that they are benefitting rather than
    suffering from political pork, the less there is public outrage at
    their graft.

    That nearly half the US population pays no taxes at all, yet receives
    thousands or even tens of thousands of dollars annually in benefits,
    while the top 10% pay more than 50% of the taxes while receiving
    nothing in return is indicative of the drift from a free market to an
    odd sort of upside down patron/client socialist/mercantilist economy,
    where the productive class is enslaved by the political elites to
    subsidize the payoffs to the unproductive class that extorts the
    payoffs under theoretical threat of electoral revolt...

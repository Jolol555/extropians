       "My guess is that people don't yet realize how

    Dear Director Hollister,

    I am a member of the Cryonics Institute. I need to be
    assured that their services are available to me when
    the time comes. Your interference with their
    operations is uncalled for, represents ignorance of or
    indifference to the scientific basis and the
    life-saving intent of the practice, violates the
    fundamental right of each individual to self-defense
    and to the right to choose one's own medical care, and
    violates both the freedom of religion and the
    separation of church and state. On a more personal
    level, setting aside the broad and egregious assault
    on my human rights, it constitutes a direct threat to
    my life.

    The vast majority of individuals who are signed-up
    with cryonics organizations are among the most
    technically sophisticated people in the world. They
    see the possibilities the future holds, in stark
    contrast to the rest of the population of the
    "civilized" world, who are almost completely ignorant
    of even the basics of present day science and
    technology. This is the same science and technology
    which, since the time of Galileo, has struggled to
    promote truth and rationality against the forces of
    mysticism and prejudice. The same science and
    technology and truth and rationality has given us the
    very civilization we today celebrate and enjoy:
    unprecedented levels of material abundance, comfort,
    safety, freedom, and, to enjoy it, a healthy lifespan
    two and a half times greater than the brief and
    brutish span of the jungle from which science has
    lifted us. Everything we are, everything we have
    built proclaims that science and rationality delivers,
    mysticism and prejudice destroys.

    The same vast majority of people who consider cryonics
    to be a sham, due so not on the basis of scientific
    training, but rather on the basis of cultural
    prejudices, superstition (which they call religion),
    and a slavish adherence convention. Fine for them.
    This is their protected right, founded on this
    country's tradition of freedom of religion. No matter
    the foolishness, no matter the dogma, the funny hats,
    or the arcane rituals, as long as it's in the name of
    some "sky Santa" (ie god), it's politically pandered
    to and protected. Fine. I stand resolute in defense
    of their rights. By that same standard I absolutely
    require for myself the same right to personal belief
    and practice. I place my faith in science and
    rationality. I do not reject religion for any who
    care to believe, but I most strongly protest when my
    preference of science in favor of religion threatens
    to make me a victim of those whose religious
    prejudices conflict with my rationalist preferences.
    And that is precisely what you are up to, Director
    Hollister, in your present assault on the Cryonics
    Institute.

    I do not know if your action arises out of political
    pandering to the religious majority, personal
    religious prejudice, a lack of technical
    sophistication, or if it is simply a bureaucratic
    blunder, but you need to stop it now, and return to
    looking for something socially constructive to do. I
    dare say there are plenty of REAL problems out there
    that could use your attention.

    If you need someone to help you understand what
    cryonics is about--and judging from the actions you've
    taken, I'd say you do, and desperately--I offer my
    assistance. But I prefer to make this "offer" more
    forcefully. You are, either capriciously or in an
    uninformed fashion, carrying out what amounts to a
    life-threatening assault on a minority whose beliefs
    differ from yours and those of the social majority.
    As such, I feel entitled to require that my views be
    heard in this matter. You have already violated the
    legally protected rights of others. The proper course
    of action now must be to cease and desist, and to make
    yourself available to those who can provide you with
    the information needed to understand why what you have
    done is a violation of rights, and how in future to
    adhere to the principle of protection rather than
    violation of rights.

    I am not the only person qualified too help you in
    this matter, but I believe that my depth of experience
    with cryonics makes me among the most skilled
    proponents of the practice.

    You will find my personal information below. Please
    contact me as an expert in these matters--a
    "cryoethicist" if you will--and notify me as to any
    public hearings, commissions of inquiry, and most
    importantly any legal proceedings related to this
    matter.

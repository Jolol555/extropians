    INteresting subject. I am also fairly sure that a trend of increasing
    agnosticism started to die sometime in the 60s. It may seem strange now, but
    in those days there were actually movies that seriously questioned the
    existence of a god, and that had characters who were atheists. Look at the
    movie The Sandpiper starring Burton and Taylor. This was not an art house
    film. That has all died out now.

    I suspect it has something to do with the consolidation of news and
    entertainment productions. You have huge conglomerations that produce news
    and films and TV shows. They are highly profit driven. This may have been
    also influenced by the movement of so many people to the cities, and at teh
    same time, the dying out of almost all of the newspapers in cities, except
    for one winner.

    In a way, our media has become like our presidential politics--aimed to the
    lowest common denominator, which is the religious believer. And so this
    stance reinforces itself.

    But I think the internet will gradually reverse the current trend. Already it
    is taking time away from TV and movies.

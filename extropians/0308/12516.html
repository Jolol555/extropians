    The Register is a well respected and irreverent hardcore technology and
    politics internet publication. However, it has a pretty large readership.
    It's articles are often quoted and referenced in online blogs. I would not
    be surprised if this article gets some exposure in online blogs such as
    metafilter or plastic. From there a couple of print outlets may pick up this
    article.

    The vicious tone of the article will prevent it from being picked up by more
    mainstream publications.

    However, given the mediagenicity of this terrorism market story, as evidenced
    by the degree of exposure we have seen for it recently, combined with
    the "sexy" tie-in to extropianism and cryonics (Hanson is signed up) I would
    expect that one or more major print outlets will soon do a followup, and
    cleaned up, article based on tying together the terror market and
    extropianism and cryonics. Triple threat mediagenicity, there. Batten down
    the hatches, etc.

    I must say that some of the earlier media exposure and websites published by
    the extropians leave them (and us cryos) wide open for articles like the
    Register piece. For example, the handshake referenced in the article, plus
    the "Best do it so" stuff. Talk about exposing your flank....

    An interesting looking pop sci book I've just started reading is EMOTIONS
    REVEALED: Understanding Faces and Feelings, by Professor Paul Ekman, who
    has researched an adjacent field for some 40 years. He started with a
    routine belief in social construction of emotions, and was shocked by his
    cross-cultural studies into accepting that emotions are human
    universals--although, a key point, their *public expression* often varies
    strikingly due to culturally contingent `display rules'.

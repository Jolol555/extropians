    [quote from: spudboy100@aol.com on 2003-08-13 at 17:40:22]
    Well, I believe that the direction of the list is conformity. And also, it
    becomes a mutual admiration society. This is quite typical of many lists, and
    very human; but hardly, transhuman.

    You could say that about any group or forum that maintains any greater degree
    of focus than, say, an unmoderated newsgroup or Slashdot in -1 mode.

    I, for one, have no bones conforming to something I agree with in the first
    place and have agreed with before I even knew there was a name for it.

    Mike is right about a bunch of things, but all too many of them are noisy,
    contentious 20th century brick-and-mortar issues that really are less
    important to the fate of humanity than what's on-topic for this list. In the
    year 2XXX, will the name or flag of any contemporary nation have any meaning
    outside the field of history? Will scarcity-era "capitalism" be any less
    laughable than scarcity-era "socialism"?

    Diversity of opinion is important. So, let's start a
    Paleo-Libertarian/Conservative Extropian list and outsource all debate
    regarding non science/technology/memetics topics to there. If there is demand,
    lets also start a corresponding Liberal/Socialist Extropian list. And leave
    this list to people who put the word Extropian first and the modifiers second
    (or absent).

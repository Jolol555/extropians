    ### This was a classical experiment with radioactively labeled
    deoxyglucose (if I remember correctly) being taken up while the monkey
    looked at a spiderweb grid (actually a target used in ophthalmology to
    analyze visual fields). The nerve cells activated by the target burned
    more energy, and took up the label more than inactive cells. Indeed you
    could see a somewhat distorted but easily identifiable grid in their
    primary visual cortex.

    However, we have to remember that only a very small number of neurons,
    if any, in the primary visual cortex, is correlated with conscious
    experience.

    > One morning I started counting up. The Edens choked down to

    This is quite true. A decade ago, I was involved in the opposition to
    the light rail project in Seattle. Studying past mass transit projects
    around the country, I found that proponents generally overestimated
    actual new ridership by 50%, and underestimated actual investment costs
    by a similar proportion. Adjusting Seattle Transit Authority's
    proposals for these facts, I found that the cost per new rider for the
    system expansion came to over $25,000 per year for the 40 year life of
    the project. Projected increased ridership was 34,000, adjusted to
    17,000, while project cost was originally around $7 billion, adjusted
    up to over $13 billion.

    Mass transit is only viable in areas which have already been radically
    transformed by other socialist policies, like fuel and other taxes,
    oppressive parking enforcement, and restrictions on construction of
    private parking facilities. Successfully used public mass transit is a
    paramount sign of a mature and ingrained socialist society.

    Because in 1991 virtually nobody (including me)
    thought Saddam could remain in power more than
    a few weeks, a month at most, after such a humiliating
    military defeat. We were absolutely certain he would be
    deposed from within and we were dead wrong.
    John K Clark

    I thought the same, and I was dead wrong.
    The truth? Fears, fears, of course.
    At the end of Desert Storm there was a
    revolt, remember? Iraqi Curds and Iraqi Sciits
    against Saddam and his guards. And US Gov. (and many
    other western Govs. too) had fears. About a possible
    gigantic extension of the revolt in Iraq, Turkey
    (Curds)and Iran (Sciits). And Saddam, and his guards,
    stopped all that.

    >
    > Whew! The first time I read that I thought you
    > said 10^2 years. Thank goodness it's really
    > ten thousand years! That was a close one!

    It's not as far off as I'd like. Imagine this. Human civilization as
    we know is destroyed by some cataclysm that doesn't wipe out the species
    or most higher life on Earth. We always have a chance to climb back up
    the ladder again, but some later catastrophies might wipe all all life
    or all higher life making this impossible. This kind of one-two punch
    is perhaps unlikely, but I'd like to see smooth sailing for the next
    several hundred thousand years or more.:) (Wasn't there a report about
    a year ago of a potential NEO hitting Earth in around 900 years?
    Imagine, if you will, our civilization experiencing a decline similar to
    the dark ages of old. That could last well over 900 years -- putting us
    in a very precarious situation as a species.)

    Of course, if humanity (or posthumanity) migrates to space or develops
    some other means of countering these threats in the interim -- which,
    barring such catastrophes, I think is very likely -- then my tolerances
    widen.:) Until we have such, though, we are extremely vulnerable.

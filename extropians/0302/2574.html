    ### If people really need trains and buses, they
    > don't need to be subsidized. Rafal

    Truer words are seldom spoken. Often the traffic
    is clogged by subsidized city buses with one or
    two passengers aboard. I notice this while I
    am stopped waiting for a train that passes by
    with one or two, and sometimes no passengers aboard.

    Universal truth: as long as there are parking
    spaces, people will drive their cars. If the
    goal is to force mass transit, the solution is
    very simple: build highrises on all existing
    parking spaces.

    Of course, forcing mass transit is not the
    *goal*, moving people to their destination is
    the goal. But politicians often lose sight
    of this fact.

    spike

    I disagree with Greg. I don't see the publicity as all that
    damaging. It clearly shows a couple of fly-by-night operators
    with agendas operating in a less-than-forthright manner.

    It was an ESPN publication and we all know how that is
    clearly the best source of high quality data.

    Worst case -- we get a few less sports oriented people
    preserving themselves cryonically. Since by the time
    they are likely to be revivied "sports" as we know them
    seem unlikely to be of much interest I don't exactly
    view this as a significant loss.

    The glass may be half full rather than half empty.

    R.

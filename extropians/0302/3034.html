    There is one thing I did learn about speed reading that I have found
    valuable throughout my life. It involves the proper way to read a work
    of non-fiction (it works for fiction also, but rather spoils the fun of
    it).

    In order:

    1. Read the preface and introduction very carefully.

    2. Read the table of contents and make a sincere effort to understand
    the meaning of each chapter and how each chapter relates to each other
    chapter.

    3. Read the first and last paragraph of each chapter, to get a better
    understanding of the chapters. Paraphrase, in your mind, the basic theme
    of each chapter. Again review the table of contents and consider how
    each chapter relates to each other chapter.

    4. If there is a conclusion to the book, read the conclusion carefully.

    5. *Then* go ahead and read (or speed-read) the book.

    People typically just dive in at page one without really understanding
    what it is they're reading. The formula above improves comprehension at
    any reading speed.

    -gts

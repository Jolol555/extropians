    -- Emlyn O'regan <oregan.emlyn@healthsolve.com.au> wrote:
    " Why isn't the US going to war with Iceland, immediately? I don't
    think that the doves on this list have provided any solid reasons for
    not going to war with Iceland. And who is any of us to say what the
    terrible implications of not going to war with Iceland might be?
    Iceland says it doesn't have any WMD, but is there proof? "

    Last I heard, Icelanders have not invaded Greenland, used WMD against
    either their own people or against the Orkneys, Greenlanders,
    Newfoundland (and we all know those radical fundamentalist Newfies
    deserve it), or the Canary Islands.

    The Icelanders, despite decades of tapping geothermal energy sources,
    have never tried to turn them into weapons, and have not tried to
    purchase technology for doing so from other countries, either openly or
    through front companies.

    The Icelanders are rather happily members of NATO and have welcomed US
    troops on their soil for decades (been there myself) rather than spend
    their vast bounties and profits from fish sales on building up their
    own military machine.

    The Icelanders have not tried to build a supergun capable of putting
    sheep into space from parts shipped under labels claiming they are for
    fish processing, nor have they bought thousands of artillery pieces
    from those dreaded Australian weapons brokers. They haven't built
    missiles capable of carrying WMD to long ranges in violation of UN
    restriction while claiming they are simply a new tool for whale
    hunting.

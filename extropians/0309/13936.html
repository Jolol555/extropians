    > about those who purchase their oil. What is wrong with us?

    There's a better reason that that. The oil is nearly gone. Current
    estimates say that 2001 (2000?, 2002?) was probably the highest year for
    pumping oil from the ground that will ever happen. And getting the
    first half of the oil out is a lot cheaper than getting the second half
    of the oil out. So look for oil prices to start climbing fast. Until
    it becomes expensive enough that oil shales are profitable. Then we'll
    have awhile to adapt, and the higher oil prices will make alternative
    more affordable (by comparison). But higher oil prices will raise the
    price of EVERYTHING. Ouch!

    And that's probably why we invaded the middle east. To buy us a few
    more years. Doesn't make it ethical, but makes it a bit more
    understandable. (It's also why France got so pissed off at us. They
    had made arrangements to acquire that oil, and we stole it. Were I
    France, I'd be pissed off too. But their arrangements were only
    slightly more ethical than ours. Well, maybe twice as ethical. But
    still pretty far from any base line.)

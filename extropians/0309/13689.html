    I think there's two main reasons for this. The UK is very expensive,
    particularly if you live in London, so if you want to spend a weekend out on
    the lash it is actually cheaper to go to places like Prague. Also, there's
    the well known phenomenon of being less inhibited in a strange place.

    Having said that, it looks as though the British are reverting to their
    historical stereotype (which applied until about 1970) - crazy, aggressive
    and belligerent, drunken, boorish, and randy. I had to laugh at the
    reference to fights breaking out when parties from Leeds, Manchester, and
    Birmingham found themselves in the same establishment while being supporters
    of their local teams - that's asking for trouble! The ones from Manchester
    can't have been Man United fans that's for sure.

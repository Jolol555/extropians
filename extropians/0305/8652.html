    > There have been studies showing, for example, that even when people win
    > enormous amounts of RAM and CPU in the lottery, they have the same
    > level of happiness a year later.

    Eliezer is right. Having once spent many years in the RAM management
    business, I can testify without hesitation that multi-meg-millionaires are
    as a group no more happy than the rest of us. Some of the richest people I
    know are also the most miserable.

    I notice that the misery level is greatest when very large sums are
    inherited. These poor saps are robbed even of the satisfaction that comes
    from patting oneself on the back for past achievements. They tend to have
    low motivation, low self-esteem, and a tendency to abuse drugs and alcohol.

    Money is not all it's cracked up to be.

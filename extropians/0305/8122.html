    --- Charles Hixson <charleshixsn@earthlink.net> wrote:
    > That's why they want it to be illegal to label food to reveal whether
    > it is GM or not. I don't know the current status of the various
    > laws, but because the market forces would act against them, the
    > conspire to conceal which foods have been GM, or irradiated, or
    > etc. (I don't feel that conspire is too strong a word... they act
    > in secret in concert with others, and do that which if the
    > beneficiaries weren't legislators would be called bribery.)
    > I'm not arguing whether the market reaction would be reasonable or
    > not. Sometimes, frankly, it seems to me unreasonable.

    So long as you apply the term consistently to describe the dealings of
    masked luddite saboteurs, of luddite special interest groups, and of
    organic industry special interest groups.

    To exclude GM but to allow hybridized plant species is a double
    standard that has no scientific basis. Hybridization is a shotgun
    method of forced evolution. GM is more like a rifle. Which is more
    likely to cause collateral damage?
     
    > But to conceal the information necessary to allow the market forces
    > to act removes one from having "market forces" used legitimately in
    > one's defense. So I don't feel that it is legitimate to defend them
    > with that argument. You may well find other arguments, but the
    > companies themselves have so acted as to render that argument
    > invalid.

    The problem, Charles, is that this is a double standard. Luddite groups
    conspire in secret, they lie, cheat, sabotage, and spread
    disinformation throughout the 'organic' community, so much so that
    there are people on this list that believe their lies. Monsanto and
    other for-profits are not permitted to act similarly because they are
    deemed to be tainted by a profit motive.

    We in this group could act against luddism, but we don't. Pro-Act is
    stillborn and every suggestion I've made in the last several years has
    been shot down by naysayers on this list.

    >> You have gotten to the crux of the your misunderstanding.
    >> The ordinary American in overwhelming numbers does care about
    >> international law. They have long been devoted to the love of
    >> international law.

    You may be right that Americans favor the /concept/ of
    international law. But you might try asking the survey question
    this way: do you favor giving planetwide control to an organization
    composed of delegates most of whom were appointed by governments
    that do not represent the interests of their people?

    The UN charter is fundamentally based on the idea that oppressive
    governments, military dictatorships, inherited monarchies, and other
    non-representative governments are legitimate and entitled to a
    voice in international affairs. Any talk of "human rights" is an
    afterthought; mere lip service from an organization that serves to
    legitimize and perpetuate tyranny accross the world.

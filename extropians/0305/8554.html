    You call those puny little rocks "worlds"? Most of them aren't even round.
    They don't have enough water. The don't have little atmospheres. They
    aren't on the edge of the solar system, leading to the stars. The entire
    asteroid belt contains about 1% of the real-estate of the Kuiper belt. :-)

    On a more serious note, this article was talking about a billion years from
    now when the sun has expanded and burned up the Earth and the Pluto zone
    would be warmed up to earth-like temperature and sunlight levels. I guess
    if you're impatient, you can move to the asteroids now. But rest assured
    that this will just be a temporary location of a billion years before the
    outer ring looks more attractive.

    (OK, maybe I just have a thing for the Pluto belt. What's not to like?
    "Planet-X", Cthulhu, turning habitable by itself, interstellar spaces,
    interplanetary outlands, undiscovered planets, etc. It seems like the
    perfect setting for a sci-fi future. The asteroids are so last century.)

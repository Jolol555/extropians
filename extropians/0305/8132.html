    This is the most important point. Heterosexual men can get AIDS in America,
    so they should wear a condom to protect themselves. However, Fumento often
    implies that heterosexual men can't get AIDS, implying that they don't need
    to wear condoms. His book title is misleading. (Heterosexual AIDS is not a
    myth, the heterosexual epidemic in America might be overhyped.) Fumento
    also explains African AIDS by saying most of the men lie about having gay
    sex. That is, he still implies that they must be gay or they wouldn't have
    gotten AIDS. Although he seems careful not to state this directly, or
    directly tell heterosexual men in America not to bother wearing condoms, he
    comes close to implying this position often. As a scientific debunker of
    pseudoscience, he seems awfully careful not to offend his conservative
    financiers.

    In other areas, he also claims that Gulf War Syndrome, Agent Orange, DDT,
    Radon Gas, Asbestos, the Atkins Diet, Nutritional Supplements, and even the
    need for fetal stem cell research are all liberal myths and lies. He has
    debunked so many things, that it is really rare to find anyone who agrees
    with all his claims.

    Brian Atkins wrote,
    > As opposed to NNTP (news), there normally aren't many hops in email
    > delivery. In fact it usually flows directly from the sender's ISP to the
    > recipient's ISP. If it were all people in Australia using a particular
    > ISP then yes, it could be that ISP's fault. But since we have multiple
    > people with all different ISP's reporting the problem, it almost
    > certainly is an issue with the originating ISP (aka Javien).

    Agreed. However, in this case, Javien provides a couple of internal hops of
    their own during the processing of Extropian List messages. Thus, in
    addition to the direct sending and receiving by ISPs:

            (for example)
            I sent the message at 11:55 local time.
            I received it from the List at 13:17 local time.

    We also see internal Javien hops during processing:

            (for example)
            Received: from tick.javien.com (javien2-3.spots.ab.ca [209.115.169.3])
                    by spool6.valueweb.net (8.12.9/8.9.2) with ESMTP id h4UHHNOF021532
                    for <mail@HarveyNewstrom.com>; Fri, 30 May 2003 13:17:27 -0400
            Received: (from majordom@localhost)
                    by tick.javien.com (8.11.6/8.11.2) id h4UFvhd06358
                    for extropians-outgoing; Fri, 30 May 2003 09:57:43 -0600

    So the final timeline is clearly documented:
    ex
            11:55 sent message to ValueWeb
            11:55 ValueWeb sent message to Extropians List
            11:?? <unknown internal processing>
            11:57 Javien queues message for "extropians-outgoing"
            13:17 Javien transmits message to my ISP at ValueWeb
            13:17 My client downloads the message from ValueWeb to my workstation

    The Javien output queue is taking an hour to an hour-and-a-half to deliver
    outgoing Extropians-List messages. This same pattern appears on other
    e-mails I am examining as well. During that period the server sent out 18
    messages. If we have more than 300 subscribers, Javien would appear to be
    delivering more than one e-mail per second to the Extropians List
    subscribers.

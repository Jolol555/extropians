    Science Daily has so much progress today, I don't know where to begin.

    - Brain seems to reset biological clock based on temperature,
      possible applications for better solutions for jet lag.
      http://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2003/07/030715090727.htm

    - Demonstration that the use of RNAi (RNA interference) can work
      as a therapy to reduce telomerase activity in cancer cells (and
      may end up developing into an anti-cancer therapy).
      http://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2003/07/030715090345.htm

    - Stem cell loss appears to contribute to atheroclerosis development.
      http://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2003/07/030715090300.htm
      [Side note: Gradual stem cell loss may be a consequence of
      defects in the overall genetic program, particularly DNA repair
      over decades of life.]

    - Gene to protect against Irish Potato Blight found (leading to it
      becoming more and more difficult to resist GMO foods... with or
      without labels!).
      http://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2003/07/030715090635.htm

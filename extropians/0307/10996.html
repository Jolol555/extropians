    > I don't see how you get to your result from 1 and 2.

    I've not included any of the reasoning here; basically, it is "insert
    materialist argument X". When I talk about "I", I refer to the fundamental
    sense of self that I assume we all experience, which seems to have no
    information processing component, yet is accessible to the information
    processing component (because I can talk about it!). I can't find any role,
    or mechanism, for a non-information processing component to consciousness.
    The materialist point of view would be, I think, that it therefore cannot
    exist (or at least can't be part of the model?).

    >
    > Why not posit: "I" whatever "I" am, am party a self referential
    > processes or even set of processes. The "I" may not be a singular
    > process it may be a plurality. Perhaps some of these processes
    > contained in what we term crudely call the "self" are recursively
    > aware of the other processes in the "self".

    I have no doubt that the information processing aspect of self aware systems
    works like this. The biggest problem, imo, is that it doesn't imply a sense
    of self like that experienced by other humans (or me, at least). You could
    posit a self aware intelligent system without a "consiousness", without it
    being a zombie bicycle.

    In short, there is something in there, the sense of "I", which shouldn't be
    there, yet which is the source of the fundamental axiom underpinning all my
    goal systems, the basis for everything that the mundane, information
    processing aspects of me strive for.

    >
    > We don't doubt we are multicellular though we talk of having
    > a body. Perhaps consciousness is a sets of precesses and to
    > refer to the self as though it is a singular thing is to make
    > a similar
    > mistake as to assume our body is a single thing.

    I can't explain even one sense of self, let alone many of them.

    In the case of the body, the deconstruction explanation works; it doesn't
    leave anything out. Vitalism doesn't cut the mustard. However, consciousness
    is different, because it is something; in some important sense it is the
    only thing. You can't leave it out.

    > The level of
    > abstraction at the body level and the "self" level losses too much
    > detail. Maybe.

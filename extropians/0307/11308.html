    > 90 light years from Earth, the Sol-like star HD70642
    > has a 2x Jupiter mass planet orbiting in a roughly
    > circular orbit at 3.3 AU from the star with no large
    > planets detected in orbits closer to the sun.
    snip...
    > This begins to set some hard numbers on some of the parameters
    > of the Drake Equation (e.g. f_p and n_e) and is likely to present
    > some problems to the "Rare Earth" proponents as well as those

    I would predict that what it means is that a) species transcend too
    fast to achieve what we regard as 'interstellar flight' of the space
    opera variety, or b) interstellar travel is just too damn difficult
    with too little return on investment for any intelligent species to
    waste money on.

    My c) prediction is that there are already a number of transcended
    alien nanocivilizations living in Who-ville in various crypto-dirt-like
    locations on our planet. They've been here a long time and regard us
    right now to be just dangerous enough that they don't want us to know
    of their existence, where they once allowed some of their kind to play
    games hacking peoples heads, producing paranormal experiences. They now
    treat this as a crime in their own civilizations.

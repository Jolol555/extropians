    http://www.theage.com.au/articles/2003/07/03/1057179090648.html

    Astronomers have discovered a planetary system resembling our own solar
    system, which could harbour an Earth-like planet. The discovery was made by
    a team of astronomers, including two Australians, using the
    Anglo-Australian Telescope at Siding Spring, near Coonabarabran in NSW. The
    Anglo-Australian Planet Search team, which has been looking for planetary
    systems like our own for five years, says that among the 100 found so far,
    this is the most similar formation. What they found was a Jupiter-like gas
    planet with a Jupiter-like elliptical orbit, with no bigger planets closer
    to its star. [etc]

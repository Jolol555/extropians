    Then we have the linguistics of transhumanism (ultraviolet linguistics? :-)
    While we hardly have a need for hiding our identity (most of the
    time) we seem to use some of our jargon as ways of detecting other
    transhumanists. A few here might remember the hotel foyer at Extro 3
    when the arrival of some Swedes talking nanotech caused almost a dozen
    of previously isolated transhumanists to crystalize around the nucleus.

    The problem is of course that many terms are getting too mainstream
    (just look at my terminology page! how much has changed since those
    days!), so we invent new catchphrases not just for new concepts, but to
    stand out.

    On Thu, 24 Jul 2003, Robert J. Bradbury wrote:

    > Someone (spike?)
    > should calculate what the LN2 costs would be to keep a body
    > frozen in a dewar on an annual basis assuming everything were
    > automated.

    The current 4 person dewars boil about 15 liters/day of LN2...call it 4
    liters/person/day. Larger dewars could easily get it to 1
    liter/person/day. Industrial quantities of LN2 run about $.05/liter, so
    you are looking at a nickel per day, or about $20/year.

    Of course, even today, LN2 costs are a fraction of the total upkeep cost.
    But the marginal cost for the million and first person could be extremely
    small.

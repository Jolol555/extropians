    At 01:29 PM 7/20/03 -0700, Lee wrote:

    >Natasha, what ever you are contemplating, I hope that
    >it is nothing excessive, and that you don't allow Harvey
    >to egg you on into doing something extreme.

    Haha. Listen, no one thinks for me. I tend to contemplate stuff for
    months, even years, before I actually do anything.

    >I don't care what other organizations or activities you
    >want to sponsor or engage in, but I deeply care about
    >any radical changes to the nature of the Extropians forum,
    >and that would be a real shame.
    >
    >I would urge others to speak up and their views be known,
    >before it is too late---or else risk that what we take for
    >granted may be no more.

    After our board meeting, anything I have in mind will be open to all ExI
    members, and non-members, and anyone can make a suggestion.

    The bottom line is that ExI needs to make a change and I am working on ways
    to ignite the change. This is not new and certainly has been discussed for
    a couple of years. Acting too quickly can be as counter-productive as
    acting too slowly. Whatever changed ExI makes will be for the benefit of
    extropic thinking, and there will be no ulterior motives. Again, the
    bottom line is that "critical extropic thinking" is the one key element
    that is sorely needed in the world and I believe we are the people to
    market it.

    >P.S. I am very sorry to hear about your debilitating
    >health problems. I wish you all the best.

    Gee, thanks. I'm doing very well and the bladder cancer is under
    control. I still work out as much as possible and my muscles are getting
    back into shape. The only problem is that I sometimes am incontinent, but
    that would have happened in a few years anyway :-)

    Mike,
           I think if you read Barnett's analysis in March's Esquire as the model
    for the present US administration's view of the world and what is to be done
    I think you will find they have a different set of assumptions.
           If I understand them they believe that certain 3rd world nations have
    to be brought into the community of nations. Again, if I understand them
    these nations should remain independent nations and equal members of the
    community.
           If I am right in my interpretation of what is going on in terms of
    Hernando de Soto's The Mystery of Capital, what is required is a basic change
    whose nature puts the various 3rd world nations on a trajectory to becoming
    1st world countries.
           Of course DeSoto believes the key change is to establish a legal
    system that guarantees private property.
           Over the weekend I was reading an editorial that seemed to indicate
    that W was leaning towards establishing a secular school system such as we
    have. I have no idea whether this would be in conjunction with DeSoto's
    proposed solution or not.
           In any case if John Taylor Gatto is correct a school system like ours
    would eventually totally remodel their society into a secular one.
    Ron h

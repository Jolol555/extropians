    --- Greg Burch <gregburch@gregburch.net> wrote:
    >
    > My question is this: What are the best strategies for addressing SARS
    > in the short, intermediate and long terms?

    How paranoid are you? How paranoid do you want to be? I could, for
    example, recommend an NBC tested gas mask from Northern Tool &
    Equipment Supply for $179 that will protect you against not only nerve
    agents like sarin and VX, blister agents like Mustard, but airborne
    radioactive dust, anthrax spores and viral agents. Walking around with
    it on may make you look wierd, but it will certainly give you a regular
    cardiovascular workout. It also works a hell of a lot better than a
    cute little hospital mask.

    SARS doesn't seem to be THAT airborne, though. Beyond close transfer of
    expectorant, touching, directly or indirectly, seem to be the primary
    means of communicability, as well as via common plumbing.

    Based on this, don't venture to areas of high density, or where public
    ideas of personal space are very compact. While American's ideas of
    personal space runs to about 36-72 inches, middle easterners and asians
    are comfortable with 12-24 inches. This could be a contributing factor
    to its spread overseas. Wear gloves, since whatever THEY touch, could
    be carrying the virus for at least several minutes afterward..

    Get rooms in the top floors of hotels, and don't use public restrooms.

    If you HAVE to ride the subway or walk the streets of Hong Kong or
    Beijing, though, buy the gas mask.

    I also hear that Singapore Airlines is the best trained and disciplined
    about identifying SARS infected travelers as well as keeping airliners
    clean. Recommended you fly with them.

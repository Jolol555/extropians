    From: Harvey Newstrom [mailto:mail@HarveyNewstrom.com]
    > I had a lot of worry that having to build all my own proteins
    > from scratch would be harder on my body.

    Your body is constantly turning over all of the proteins inside of it.
    Protein turnover actually *slows* with age, allowing greater build-up
    of damaged proteins. Animals whose lifespans have been slowed by
    various methods have shown a higher rate of protein turnover at old
    age than normally aging animals.

    So data suggests to me that faster protein turnover is more closely
    associated with maintaining health and youth.

    re: list of mail-order sources of range-fed and wild-game meat and omega-3
    enriched eggs

    I've received several requests for this list in private e-mail so I thought
    I would post it.

    Wild game is of course the most paleolithic. Range-fed meats come close. The
    corn-fed hormone-injected beef from the local supermarket is least like the
    meats upon which we evolved, though personally I'm usually content to buy
    lean cuts and trim any fat.

    I often marinate my steaks in virgin olive oil and red wine, sometimes with
    some additional spices. I would consider using flaxseed or cornola oil in my
    marinade, both of which are even better than olive oil with respect to the
    omega 3/6 balance, but I like the flavor of olive oil and it's hard to argue
    with the Mediterraneans who are so healthy on a diet rich in olives and
    olive oil. There may be some as yet undiscovered compounds in paleolithic
    olives that help to preserve health. The red wine adds more flavor, and
    possibly some antioxidant properties if the polyphenols aren't destroyed by
    the heat of cooking.

    These chicken farmers below sell omega-3 enriched eggs, which are
    significantly better than ordinary supermarket eggs but not exactly the same
    as eggs from range-fed chickens. Some major supermarkets already carry
    omega-3 enriched eggs for a little more than the price of ordinary
    non-enriched eggs produced by the larger farmers. True range-fed chickens
    eat mostly insects; their eggs and meat provide an indirect but palatable
    way for the paleodieter to get some of the possible benefits of paleolithic
    insect foods. I'm aware of people who eat only the eggs from their own
    range-fed chickens but I'm not aware of any commercial sources for them.

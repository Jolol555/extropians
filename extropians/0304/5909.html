    > I found the following article very interesting and
    > straightforward about the real reasons for the war in Iraq and
    > other foreign policy decisions of this administration. I will
    > be very curious to see reactions to this and how many people
    > believe this sort of policy is actually a good thing.

    http://www.sptimes.com/2003/04/06/Worldandnation/_Neocons__espouse_pre.shtml

    I don't think the article discusses any real reasons for the war.
    Instead it describes the evolution of an attitude that has developed in
    certain policy circles that the time has come to stop pussyfooting
    about with tin pot thugs, and that all human beings deserve to live in
    free and democratic societies.

    My own opinions about the real reasons for this war, and any others to
    follow are that there are several contingencies that need to be
    prepared for:

    a) a globally muscular China by mid-century challenging US dominance
    militarily. By this time, the US will need to have most of the other
    challenges to its leadership sewn up, with as large a resource base as
    possible to deal with the threat of Chinese nationalism.

    b) transhumanist technologies can only be safely developed if the
    nations of the world are all peaceful and democratic nations of free
    peoples. In a world of nanotech, AI, etc., low trust societies cannot
    be permitted to exist. All human beings must grow up and learn to
    rationally evaluate their own rational long term self interest. This
    can only occur in free, high-trust societies.

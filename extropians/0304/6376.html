    H.G. Wells:
    No one would have believed, in the last years of the 19th century, that
    chicken affairs were being watched from the timeless restaurants across the
    road. No one could have dreamed that chickens were being scrutinized as
    someone on a farm studies animals that grow and fatten in preparation for
    slaughter. Few chickens even considered the possibility of life across the
    road. And yet, across the vast lanes of the road, minds immeasurably
    superior to the chickens' regarded their coop with hungry eyes, and slowly,
    and surely, they drew their plans against them....

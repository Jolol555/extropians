    There was nothing dishonest about my quote, Lee, and I rather resent the
    implied accusation.

    As I just finished writing to Damien Sullivan, I picked turkey breast from
    fryer-roasters because fryer-roasters are the most tender and most
    flavorable kind of turkey, and because the breast portion is typically the
    body-part that most people prefer not only for dinner but also for lunch in
    between two slices of (ahem) bread.

    A lot of people are averse to the "dark" meat from poultry and a lot of
    other people are averse to eating the skin. But few carnivorous homo sapiens
    object to the juicy and tasty skinless breast of a fryer-roaster.

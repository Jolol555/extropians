    Casteneda was, I think, an example of a "novelist" (or philosopher, or
    whatever you wanna call him) whose ideas were a potpourri of emerging
    memes, which he harvested, molded into just the right format, and
    served them up at just the right time to the general public. And got
    rich off of it.

    Another writer who came along about 20 years or so later and did much
    the same thing was Whiteley Streiber. He harvested all those UFO
    memes floating around ot there and molded them into just the right
    framework of quasi-fictional, quasi-autobiographical pseudo-religion,
    which he served up in the bookstores. And got rich, too.

    Of course, Streiber was the superior craftsman, by far.

    I don't keep up with fiction anymore, but I imagine there are a few
    writers out there right now trying to come up with the next
    collective-pop-consciouness pseudo-religion. Look for elements of
    genetic engineering (of course, Strieber already tapped that vein a
    bit, and was he ever prescient on that score).

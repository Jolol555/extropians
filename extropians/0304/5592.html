    draft of the 40103a standard for computer hardware meant to be implanted in
    the user’s body. The first version of the standard, popularly called
    “Biowire,” is meant to cover the two classes of hardware consumers are
    likely to see over the next year: “implantables” and the more flexible
    “dermal sockets.”

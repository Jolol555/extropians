    Mike Lorrey wrote:

    > It is possible to lose weight in all sorts of unsmart ways,
    > ask any concentration camp survivor.

    Yes. The problem with deli sandwiches, from my paleodiet perspective, is
    that the buns are mostly empty calories and many deli "meats" (e.g.,
    pepperoni) contain too much fat.

    Concerning the meats, this gets to a central issue regarding the Atkins
    diet: the diet is a popular success largely because it allows luxuries like
    butter and cheese and bacon and sausage, which help people stay with the
    diet by satisfying their lust for fat. (I remember how much I loved my bacon
    and cheese omelets back when I followed Atkins). But these fatty foods are
    luxuries with potentially serious health consequences. There is to my
    knowledge no good reason to include fatty meats like sausage, bacon and
    pepperoni, much less butter and cheese. If an Atkins dieter decides he
    absolutely cannot live without his fatty butter and cheeses then he might
    still improve on the diet by eating only lean cuts of meat, fish and
    poultry.

    On Thu, 2003-04-10 at 12:42, matus wrote:
    > I am in absolute shock and awe that no one felt the events of yesterday were
    > worthy of mention. Here the symbolic downfall of one of the worst murderous
    > dictators in the world took place, and people held under his oppresion
    > rejoiced and desicrated the stalinistic reminders of his murderous rule.
    >

    ### Yes, these events are by all means worthy of mention. You might
    remember that I was very mildly against the war about three weeks ago,
    mainly for financial reasons. Now, I see the joy of Iraqis, and for all
    my stingy impulses, I have a hard time refusing to think our 10e11
    dollars might be money well-spent. The whole situation might still come
    to haunt the US in one way or another, since it ain't over until it's
    over, and it's never over, either, but right now I remember the feeling
    I had in 1989, or 1990, when the much milder but still onerous socialist
    regime in Poland was going down, and I felt quite happy about it.

    Let's hope peace in Iraq will be as successful for the good guys as the
    war was.

    Rafal

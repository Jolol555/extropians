    I was going to make an ad hominem here, comparing Samantha's grip on
    reality to that of Baghdad Bob. I think, instead, that her attitude
    here reflects a common difference in attitude.

    I get generally categorized with conservatives, despite my many
    libertarian opinions, but I think that this labelling occurs because I
    share a neocon attitude about my opponents.

    Liberals and left wingers, and their sympathizers, generally have an
    attitude that their enemies and opponents cannot possibly have
    benevolent intentions because they are inherently evil, wrong, and
    stupid. They beleive that because libertarians like myself and neocons
    tend toward beleiving in the value of self interest, that that is ALL
    we value, that we cannot possibly have any fiber of altruism in our
    beings, they cannot consider the idea that self sacrifice can serve
    self interest.

    Conversely, libertarians like myself and many neocons have an attitude
    about liberals and other left wingers like Samantha that they certainly
    do have benevolent intentions, but that the policies they espouse to
    achieve them are inherently evil, wrong, and stupid, SPECIFICALLY
    because they reject the value of self interest as a goal of self
    sacrifice.

    --- Brian Booth <brian_booth1@yahoo.dk> wrote:
    >
    > I have noticed on the list that anybody who critisizes american
    > foreign policy is immediately branded anti American, I think people
    > from the states must realize that America like many other countries
    > ocassionally make a foreign policy clanger.

    Perhaps, but to be uniformly against (i.e. anti-) US foreign policy
    (i.e. -American) as a matter of course, one cannot help but quack like
    the duck se is. Since 78% of Americans fully support Bush's policy on
    Iraq, you are expressing your opposition to the opinion of more than
    3/4 of the American people. Since that is more than enough of a
    supermajority to amend our own constitution, it should be a given that
    if you are against the policy we Americans agree with by such a large
    margin, then you are against us, ergo anti-American.

    > I notice that Micheal
    > Dickey says that there are people more in the know than I am that
    > Iraq was a threat to the states,well the war is almost over and up to
    > yet no WMD have been found...

    This is incorrect. Much has been found already. We are analysing it in
    the lab to affirm by incontrovertible scientific evidence, not just the
    sketchy affirmation of a quick and dirty field test. I expect the
    report, when released, will rather roundly condemn the UNSCOM and its
    inspection process as a fraud perpetrated by the very nations that
    aided Iraq in its crimes. However, I will wait on this before making
    this an absolute prediction.

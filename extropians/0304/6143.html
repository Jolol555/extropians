    --- Hal Finney <hal@finney.org> wrote:
    > Are there any Extropians who support the Kyoto protocols, which would
    > decrease CO2 levels marginally at considerable cost? Wouldn't it
    > make more sense to wait until we have decent technology, when we can
    > cheaply achieve much more meaningful changes?

    Of course it would. By then we'll also know if CO2 actually has the
    impact some claim it does and whether it is actually possible to
    seriously alter the climate using this mechanism alone, and whether
    there are not some mechanisms in the earth system that naturally
    mitigate it (like increasing plant/plankton growth).

    Those who claim something needs doing now are the same sort of
    individuals who are trapped in modes of linear thinking, incapable of
    understanding exponential growth curves. Considering that technology is
    growing exponentially, while the impact of increasing CO2 seems to
    follow diminishing returns, it becomes increasingly more profitable,
    both economically AND ecologically to wait for cheaper solutions.

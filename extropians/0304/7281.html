    I'm glad the future doesn't need us: a critique of Joy's pessimistic futurism
    by John G. Messerly
    "In his well-known piece from Wired Magazine's April 2000 edition, "Why the future doesn't need us," Bill Joy argues that 21st century technologies - genetic engineering, robotics, and nanotechnology (GNR) - will extinguish human beings as we now know them, a prospect he finds deeply disturbing. I find his arguments deeply flawed and will critique them in turn..."

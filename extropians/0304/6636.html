    Lee Daniel Crocker wrote:

    >> We really ought to eat brains more often.
    >
    > Except that now we have to deal with CJD/BSE.

    True. Actually I was half-joking... I can't imagine myself eating brains...
    but since you took me seriously I wonder if it would be safe to eat lamb or
    pork brains. According to the USDA Nutrient Database, a 100 mg serving of
    cooked pork brain has 14 mg of vitamin C. A 100 mg serving of cooked lamb
    brain has 12 mg of vitamin C. Both also contain essential omega-3 fatty
    acids so important to human brain function. Brains also contain plenty of
    protein, B vitamins and vitamin E.

    I suppose one could, for example, grind it and mix it with meat loaf. Then
    again perhaps the hurdle is only psychological. Animal brains might have a
    nice flavor. Most fatty foods do.

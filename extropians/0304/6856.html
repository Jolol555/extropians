    No, there seems to be no other like Al Stewart. I think the reason that he
    had limited success in the 70's was the was music was pidgeon-holed back
    then, for easier marketing and promotion. A mix of folk (which was dead),
    pop, and 70's light rock. Kind of a melodic Dylan. Warren Zevon came in the
    same realm as Al Stewart, and was similarly limited. Curiously, I find
    Dylan's 70's and 80's lyrics and tunes (which nobody listens to) much better
    then the popular stuff he did in the 60's. It seems pretty easy to change the
    lyrics to Dylan and Al Stewarts songs, to an Extropian bent. This is like the
    "filk-singing" of so many science fiction fans.

    Mitch

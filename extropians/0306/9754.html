    On Sun, 8 Jun 2003, Mike Lorrey wrote:

    >"Everyone is entitled to their opinion" is such a cop out. Do you think
    >that monarchists should bring the monarchy back to Italy? Perhaps the
    >Pope should rule Italy like he used to? Or the Spanish? Perhaps the
    >Nazis should run Germany once more...

    There are monarchists in Italy. They can freely speak about bringing the
    monarchy back. Actually doing so would require a modification of the
    Constitution.

    Of course, the Constitution provide ways to change itself. A complex
    procedure is required, as you can imagine :-) . Basically, 66% or more of
    both parliaments houses is required, plus a slew of OK signs from various
    sources, plus a special commission, etc. But it can be done. It has been
    done for a few issues. Some months ago, the ex-monarch Savoia family was
    allowed to enter Italy again. A Constitution change was required for this,
    it was done, and I couldn't care less.

    There are many Nazi/Fascist nostalgics. A real Fascist party is forbidden
    by the constitution, but this does not prevent Mussolini granddaugther to
    sit in the Parliament, because she was elected as part of National
    Alliance, the main right-wing party (I mean right-wing in an European
    sense). There are parties that are to all effects Fascist except the name.
    They get about 0.2% of votes. On the other side of the spectrum there is a
    Marxist-Leninist party. It also gets some 0.2% of the votes.

    All of them are allowed to partecipate, and no one refers to them as
    "enemy of Italy". Maybe they are enemy of the current political-economic
    system, and disagree about what the current government is doing - if so,
    they are in good company :-)

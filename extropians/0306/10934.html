    Mandatory "I told you so..."

    Front page L.A. Times, June 27, 2003: "U.S. Looks at
    Organizing Global Peacekeeping Force"

    Starting last year with Westercon, where Tad Daley
    <http://www.cunr.org/priorities/statement_Daley.htm>
    ran a large open discussion forum on the creation of a
    World Federation, and continuing with Daley (left
    winger) and Jerry Pournelle (right winger technocrat /
    military science fiction author / science advisory
    panel to congress / science advisor to Newt Gingrich /
    co-author of Star Wars plan) amazingly appearing
    together on a related panel at LosCon, then
    reappearing as a PBS blitz during the opening phase of
    "Iraqi Freedom," the drumbeat is continuing for the
    creation of a U.S.-led International Police Force. So
    far the cards are falling exactly as I called them
    almost a year ago.

    Note that I also predicted the Iraq war shortly after
    9/11, based on information coming from Laurie
    Melrouis, who was in charge of Iraq analysis under
    Clinton
    <http://www.nationalreview.com/comment/comment-mylroie052902.asp>

    In neither case was the information itself or the
    source particularly authoritative. However, I asked
    myself at the time, "WHY are these people speaking out
    the way they are, unless they expect to be proven
    right or prophetic?" All parties mentioned have vast
    resources of info and serious backgrounds in the
    subject matter. None of them are simple patsies
    putting out some spin line. They all have lives and
    are forces in their own rights. I.e., it was a safe
    bet that they had insider info.

    When there did not appear to be any real justification
    for the Iraq war on its own merits, I had to look
    further, assuming a longer term, larger plan. I doubt
    that Pournelle or Daley had any idea that anyone would
    put two and two together, but why else would they be
    saying the things they were, separately and, bizarrely
    - given their poles-apart politics, together. As I
    said almost a year ago, the point of the Iraq war is
    to set a precedent that will serve as a stepping stone
    for a world police system, a coalition of many nations
    providing troops, money, logistics, etc., but led by
    the U.S. and outside the U.N. or NATO.

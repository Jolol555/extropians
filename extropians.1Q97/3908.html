In response to John K. Clark's comment on cloning Stephen Hawking, giving the
clone an average type education, and seeing how the clone would possibly
still be great at physics, to answer the genetic question of genius, I'd like
to extend the thought. Imaging making ten clones on Hawking, sort of a grand
test, and raising each one with different educations, environments, etc.
Let's see how each turns out. If all goes well, we may even make a few
technological advances along the way, having ten more Hawkings around.

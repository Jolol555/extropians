> Do people think there are any non-bogus ethical considerations
> in regard to cloning? If so, what are those?

The health of the clone is of course important, so safety concerns are
valid ethical concerns.

The social concerns I mentioned in my big post have some merit: I think
you have to show that the problems are not unavoidable consequences of
the technology, which actually puts a certain burden of reasonable proof
on the pro-cloning side.

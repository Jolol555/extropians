Scientists are close to releasing a commerical model of a slug-hunting robot
that is powered by the slimy creatures it catches. The four-wheeled SlugBot
uses image sensors to tell the difference between slugs, snails and worms, and
then picks up the prey with its claw arm.
http://www.business2.com/articles/web/0,,36337,FF.html

Scientists who have been developing a slug-hunting robot that could be powered
by its prey are close to releasing a commercial model.
http://news.zdnet.co.uk/story/0,,t269-s2100781,00.html

Further research is being continued by the team based on Slugbot's self
sustaining energy autonomy, this time using plant material rather than dead
slugs.

U.S. scientists urge more research with embryos
December 02, 2001 3:14:00 PM ET
By Maggie Fox, Health and Science Correspondent
WASHINGTON, Dec 2 (Reuters) - The United States should not condemn research
that uses cloning and stem cells from embryos but should instead fund it,
some scientists said on Sunday.
The researchers, speaking at the Second Annual Conference on Regenerative
Medicine, said they aim not to kill babies or to create cloned people but
to use the body's own resources to heal itself.
"We cannot have this research blocked," Dr. William Haseltine, chairman and
chief executive officer of Human Genome Sciences Inc. (HGSI) and chairman
of the conference, said. "We need a massive infusion of federal funds."

If those comments are clear, calm, and to the point, I don't want to
read any of his more voluminous ones.

The primary problem with his whole argument is to blame every death that
has occured in the Sudan since the bombing of the pharmaceuticals plant
on the US. The fact is that those that have died due to lack of access
to medicines that would have been produced by that plant are on the
Christian side of the civil war that is waging there, and so they would
not have had any access to such medicines in the first place. Those on
the muslim side have had plenty of access to medicines through the Red
Crescent relief services. The Islamist government has obstructed Red
Cross efforts to aid Christians.

Furthermore, the US government did offer a significant amount of money
to pay for the mistake that was made. I don't know if anyone ever
accepted the money or not.

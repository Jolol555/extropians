> What irritates me the most about this situation is that this whole
> story is only in the allegation stage, with absolutely no proof other
> than this story from an ex-intern who may or may not have made up the
> whole thing to impress her coworkers.

There *is* a quite a bit of circumstantial evidence (which, despite
what Perry Mason will tell you, is often the most important and
accurate). These points have either been admitted or have
documentary proof:

1. Vernon Jordan, the President's right hand man and First Pal,
was active in finding her a job outside the White House before the
last election.

2. She was visiting the White House and receiving phone calls from
it as late as last month.

3. She was receiving packages from the White House by courier.

This is extremely unusual for a White House intern, who is at the
*bottom* of the totem pole, who might catch a glimpse of the
President 2 or 3 times in the entire time, and who are specifically
ordered to stay away from the President if they do see him.

Now this all just suggests that she and the President were having
an affair, and I will join the chorus of those who don't give a damn
what he does with his wing-wang, but it does also lend the woman
more credibility in her statements.

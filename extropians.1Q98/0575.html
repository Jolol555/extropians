Here's a little example:

I have a 'Light Doodler' gadget from Chatsworth, California, a beautiful
spinning YAGI aerial with microprocessor-controlled multicoloured LEDs set
around a 4" dia. circular polycarbonate ring. You can set the controls to
configure beautiful shifting patterns on the surface of a described 4"
sphere. It has a built-in microphone to enable it to give you a light-show
in time with music. Natasha, you'd love it!

I had this gadget set up atop my TV. Behind the TV were the covers of
Enya's first three -- magical -- albums -- 'Watermark'; 'Shepherd Moons';
and 'The Celts.'

The YAGI aerial was gently 'hunting' to and fro. I looked intently at the
first album cover. The YAGI instantly locked onto the first album cover,
"Watermark.' I thought: 'Interesting...' and then thought 'Let's see what
happens if I direct my gaze to to the second album.'

I flicked my gaze over, and the YAGI loop instantly pointed and locked
directly onto Shepherd Moons. "Hey, Ho", I thought, and flicked my gaze to
'The Celts.' Instantly, the loop pointed and locked. I carried on
like this for ten minutes or so, until it got boring doing it.

This kinda thing happens all the time in my life. When I was an atheist
skeptic, it used to drive me damn near crazy. >:-} I actually preferred
trying to think I _was_ crazy instead of simply accepting overwhelming
empirical tested and corroborated evidence. >:-}

Another little example. Three times out of three tries I've done this.

Each time, I asked a kid (around 8 or 9) if they knew about 'the Force'
in 'STAR WARS.'

"Yes!" they said.

"OK", said I, "Let's see if the Force is strong in you. Pick up this pair
of dice, close your eyes, and picture a pair of sixes."

"Yes" they said.

"OK: now throw a pair of sixes" sez I.

Each and every time, they threw a pair of sixes...

Easy-peasy... >:-}

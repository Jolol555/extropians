Damien Broderick writes:
>Despite the beautiful patterns of life, evolution *has* no plan. It is a
>gigantic, stupid lottery. In such a universe, we are freed from fears of
>impiety. Since evolution does not have a plan for us, we may choose one
>for ourselves. In fact, that is what we have always done, whether we knew
>it or not. Yet people find it hard to grasp this essential moral point.

A nit: each of us can choose our own plan, but as humanity we cannot
choose a plan for ourselves. Evolutionary selection still functions, and
plans have reproductive consequences. Those who choose plans which result
in fewer progeny will be selected out in the long term.

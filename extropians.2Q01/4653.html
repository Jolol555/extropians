Afghan Taliban Orders Non-Muslims to Wear Badge
Photos

Reuters Photo

ISLAMABAD, Pakistan (Reuters) - Afghanistan (news - web sites)'s Taliban
rulers ordered the country's non-Muslim minorities Tuesday to wear a
distinctive badge on their clothes while going outdoors.

The Taliban-controlled Voice of Shariat radio, monitored in Islamabad,
quoted religious police chief Maulawi Abdul Wali as saying the order had
been issued in the light of a fatwa, or religious decree, given by Islamic
scholars.

``The ulema (scholars) issued a fatwa that the non-Muslim population of the
country should have a distinctive mark such as a piece of cloth attached to
their pockets so they should be differentiated from others,'' it quoted Wali
as saying in an interview with the official Bakhtar news agency.

``And this decision is in accordance with the Sharia (Islamic law),'' he
said.

I am wondering how many people on this list use browsers now which
support basic HTML styling commands. Eudora Pro 3.0, Netscape, and IE
mail readers support them and I am wondering now if I am able to use
bold, italics, and underlining in my text in appropriate moments without
people complaining they're seeing a message with codes in it.

If your reader can view HTML commands:

<bold>This should be bolded.

</bold><italic>This should be italicised.

</italic><underline>This should be underlined.

</underline>I can add
<bigger><bigger><bigger><bigger><bigger>D</bigger></bigger></bigger></bigger></bigger>rop
characters.

<paraindent><param>left,out</param>I can indent.

</paraindent><center><paraindent><param>out</param>I can center.

</paraindent></center><paraindent><param>out</param><flushright>I can
right justify.

</flushright>I can even add pretty
<color><param>8080,0000,0000</param><bigger><bigger><bigger>p</bigger></bigger></bigger></color><bigger><bigger><bigger><color><param>0000,8080,0000</param>s</color><color><param>8080,8080,0000</param>y</color><color><param>0000,0000,8080</param>c</color><color><param>8080,0000,8080</param>h</color><color><param>0000,8080,8080</param>o</color><color><param>8080,8080,8080</param>d</color><color><param>c0c0,c0c0,c0c0</param>e</color><color><param>ffff,0000,0000</param>l</color><color><param>0000,ffff,0000</param>i</color><color><param>0000,8080,8080</param>c</color>
<color><param>0000,0000,ffff</param>c</color><color><param>ffff,0000,ffff</param>o</color><color><param>0000,ffff,ffff</param>l</color><color><param>0000,0000,8080</param>o</color><color><param>ffff,0000,0000</param>r</color>s!

</bigger></bigger></bigger></paraindent>I suggest we switch over to this new format if we're ready and wanting to enhance our communication as well as more stylized messages. Imagine the .sigs you could create?!?

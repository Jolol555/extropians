Ian Goddard, in a forwarded message, reports that

> The "rider", which will likely cause the eventual closure of
> health food stores across the country, is part of the United Nations
> W.H.O. Alimentarious Commission's program to control
> all food supplements worldwide. Its provisions have already been
> imposed in Norway where already half their health food stores
> have been forced to close.
>
> UN provisions under the W.H.O. program declare amounts
> of Vitamin C in excess of 250 mg, for example, to be a drug.

1. Why would anyone want to do this? Qui bono? The only way
to get a handle on this is to understand it in terms of cause and effect.

2. What is to be done? I pose that question with the same seriousness
with which Lenin originally posed it. "Vote for Harry Browne" isn't the
answer. "Escape through a wormhole into a different universe" isn't
the answer, either.

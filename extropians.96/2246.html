> On Tue, 22 Oct 1996, Chris Hind wrote:
>
> > >Gallup also did this with small children, and found that self-awareness
> > >appears during the second year of life
> >
> I can remember a little of my early childhood, and it feels rather alien
> to me. I can at least recognize some of the traits of my current self in
> my memories from five years and forward, but the earliest fragments
> belong to a very different kind of being. No doubt I will say the same
> thing in a thousand years too about this era...

I can remember much of my childhood quite clearly and have a
strong sense of continuity of self from then 'til now.

Most importantly I remember playing on the floor with some toys
and getting my first glimpses of "me".....kinda like "wait a minute" who
is that? Again, who is that? I remember being quite confused by a
simultaneous awareness of the "me" and "outside things" beginning.

Since the floor is definitely our 2nd house I must have been
around 2 years old.

I have a few memories from our first house only, but only pictures
of the stairs to the upper flat, etc.

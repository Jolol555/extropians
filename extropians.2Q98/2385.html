I'm no fan of organized religon, but in the
John Hopkins (University) Medical Letter,
a sidebar listing "Longevity Facts" states:

"Your faith may heal you. Research shows that people
who practice organized religion have a lower incidence
of depression and suicide, substance abuse, and even
hearth disease. They also seem to cope better with life-
threatening illness and recover from sickness and injury
faster than those who are not religiously committed."

John Hopkins Medical Letter, Health After 50.
Volume 10, Issue 5. July 1998, page 1.

Greg Burch writes:
> Consider the deep reservoir of animistic concepts and feelings we
> have from our primitive past.... As our property becomes infused with
> intelligence of higher and higher orders, I can see a return to this kind
> of social activity. A truly "smart car" will not require a physical key -
> or not a physical key alone. It will really recognize its owner and be
> programmed with behaviors of "loyalty" to her.

I remember reading an article with a similar theme back when I was in
college, in the mid 1970s. I think it was by Newell or Simon, one of
the AI pioneers who had worked on the "general problem solver" paradigm,
and it described the goals of their AI program at CMU.

As he put it, the ultimate goal of AI was to recreate the world of
Faerie. Lampposts would contain helpful animistic spirits that would
help travellers find their way, and so on. The world would come alive,
embued with intelligence as in the old stories.

It was a very well written essay and made me consider going into AI for
a while. I can't find it on the web but maybe a copy exists somewhere.

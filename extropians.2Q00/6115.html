_CATASTROPHISM! Man, Myth and Mayhem in Ancient History and the Sciences_,
I. Tresman, Compiler, CD-ROM, 1999. Regularly: $245.00. Special price to
SF (Science Frontiers) subscribers: $165.00

Sure, this is a big investment, but where else can you get such a massive,
searchable cornucopia of material that challenges establishment archeology,
astronomy, geology, mythology, and virtually all other sciences? This
powerful research tool contains the full texts of these off-mainstream
journals:

Aeon (1988-1997)
Kronos (1975-1988)
Pensee (1972-1974)
SIS Review (1975-1998)
SIS Workshop (1978-1995)
The Velikovskian (1993-1994)

Also: Science Frontiers (1977-1996), the book _The Works of Flavius
Josephus_, and some web-site material. If you could find and buy all
this information, the estimated cost would be $1,500.

Computer requirements: A web browser; 7 MB hard-disk space; Windows 3.1,
95, 98, or Macintosh 68K system 7.

Note: Science Frontiers is a bimonthly collection of digests of scientific
anomalies in the current literature. Published by the Sourcebook Project,
P.O. Box 107, Glen Arm, MD 21057. Annual subscription: $7.00.

ISSN 1094-8325

Zero Powers wrote:

> I see it as more than just shopping. It's all info, all the time. Want to
> see how little Johnny is doing in his kindergarten class? Look into the
> display screen on your wrist-watch/pager/cell phone.

Roger all that. Ive been lurking on a wearables list and have been toying
with the idea of a borganism kind of sitch, a global brain, where volunteers
would wear wireless camera glasses and offer one's current view of the
world to anyone else in the borg. You would remove them in your house
of course, or when you were visiting Mike Lorrey. {8-] Or when you
were in the john. Or I guess you could just not look down then. {8^D

That all-information-all-the-time business really gets me jazzed.

> To me, this whole wireless thing is just an incremental step to where we'll
> all soon want to be. I want to have it all (the whole internet) *inside* my
> head. I don't want to have to carry a cell phone or a clunky old Palm
> Pilot, and certainly not a huge laptop.

Me too. Constant connection to the internet would be like tacking
on about 30 IQ points. I could get up into the normal range. Well,
depending on where you surf of course. Might subtract 30.

> Wireless is just the first baby step. How can that *not* be a cool thing?

Sounds wicked cool to me. {8-] spike

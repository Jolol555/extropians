"J. R. Molloy" wrote:
>
> Nor does it say anything about the wisdom (or lack thereof) of pursuing machine
> intelligence at the expense of eliminating human stupidity.

Speaking as someone with fingers in both the
human-intelligence-enhancement and Artificial Intelligence pies, I think
it makes tremendous sense to pursue AI at the expense of IE, and my
efforts have been focused on the former for some time. Human IE - of
the kind I used to be interested in - means that you have to wait for
the kids to grow up before anything interesting happens, and that would
just take too long.

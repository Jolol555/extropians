It appears as if Spike Jones <spike66@ibm.net> wrote:
|
|John Lennon song: My Sweet Lord. Part of the lyrics go
|
|...Give me light, give me life, keep me free from birth...

Perhaps James Wetterau <jwjr@ignition.name.net> wrote:
|
|That song is by George Harrison, not John Lennon. Harrison's music
|company lost a lawsuit accusing him of plagiarizing the 1962 Chiffon's
|song "He's So Fine" melodically. Harrison more or less conceded at
|trial that he must have heard the song in the past and subconsciously
|been aware of the two motifs his song had in common with "He's So
|Fine". He thus lost the case for subconscious plagiarism. The
|damages portion of the case dragged on for years, and I'm not sure it
|was ever resolved completely, though it ought to have been for now.

Maybe Michael M. Butler <butler@comp-lib.org> wrote:
|
|The lyrics are from a different song on the same album, I think.

The 1991 Compact Disk ``Living In The Material World'' which contains the
song ``Give Me Love'' (a.k.a. ``Give Me Peace On Earth'' which started this
thread) does not mention the Chiffon's nor any lyrics author except for
George Harrison.

One may thus assume that lawyers have been fed, and the lawsuit(s) settled.
Let us now leave this thread to gather dust in the extropian archive vaults.

These guys have been more or less discredited on this list a while back.

As I recall, I actually went to the effort to fully read their published
lab docs on some of the catalytic chemistry experiments that "proved"
their claim. The lab procedure was a joke and only gave the appearance of
rigor. Besides some questionable lab practices, they overlooked/ignored
some important factors (including ones that could potentially explain the
"mystery" without invoking new physics or chemistry) that would be obvious
to anyone well versed in inorganic chemistry and analytical lab procedure.
Very amateurish in my opinion. I do see that they have added more
"evidence" since last time, but I'm not going to waste my time reading it.

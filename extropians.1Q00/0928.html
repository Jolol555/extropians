I'm not going to get into the pro-life/anti abortion right or wrong antics
here. But one must consider the actual stage of approval for actions such as
freezing aborted embryos. John Grigg stated something close to this, "women
who go in for abortions would not want the baby to be cryosuspended."I would
like to simplify that into: some one would have to give the authority, sign
on the dotted line. It most likely would not be a mother, who chooses this
process (mostly) within guidelines of confidentiality. If not the mother,
this suggests the physician, this would be an obvious controversy entailing
infringement rights and impending lawsuits I'm sure.

> Wrong. Remember Waco?

What about Waco? Koresh was suspected of +illegally+ manufacturing automatic
weapons, and was busted on the sale of a weapon that is regulated by the NFA of
1934 without doing transfer tax paperwork, and without a class III license to do
so. The NFA laws, for those that have actually read them, specifically state
that corporations are not required to undergo background checks, nor pay the
$200 transfer tax.. The authority of the ATF is solely based upon the NFA of
1934, which is not a gun control law, it is a tax law. The ATF is not a law
enforcement agency, it is a tax collection agency. The Koresh people were
manufacturing class III weapons without a Class III permit, and without paying
the $200.00 transfer taxes. Waco was not a law enforcement activity, it was a
tax collection mission reminiscent of something you'd see in cartoons like Hagar
the Horrible or the Wizard of Id.

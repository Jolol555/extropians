The Lancet 354: 652 August 21,1999

Abstract:

    A retrospective analysis of the health and functional status of
centenarians shows they are healthy and independent for most
of their lives and experience a relatively rapid terminal decline.

Additional quote from text of the report:

    "Functional impairment, though common at the end of their lives,
was uncommon at a mean age of 92 years when 89% of the
individuals were living independently."

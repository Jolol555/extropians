FBI critics claim that FLIR video taken over Waco reveals
automatic gunfire into the community on the last day. The
FBI counters with the claim that the flashes are not gun
shots and that FLIR can't even detect gunfire. However,
an inside source informs Matt Drudge that during FLIR
tests conducted in Waco today: "The hot flashes were
completely recreated using a sniper on the ground."

Excerpted quotation of Drudge Report:

=============================================================
XXXXX DRUDGE REPORT XXXXX SUN MARCH 19 2000 20:48:09 ET XXXXX

SOURCES: WACO RE-ENACTMENT SHOWS 'OBVIOUS GUNFIRE';
FLASHES CAPTURED ON TAPE

**Exclusive**

Rapid-fire bursts of light appearing on aerial infrared
surveillance footage made by the FBI during the 1993 showdown
at Waco were spectacularly recreated on Sunday, according to
investigators, rapid-fire bursts recreated by flashes from gunfire!

"The hot flashes were completely recreated using a sniper on
the ground," revealed a congressional source, one of 35 people
to witness the court-ordered re-enactment of the final hours
of the Branch Davidian showdown.

A second source also present at the tightly-controlled test
conducted at Fort Hood said the FBI, which claimed the '93
flashes were simply reflections of sunlight off debris and
water, has been dealt a setback on Sunday.

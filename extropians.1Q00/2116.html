On Saturday, February 05, 2000 7:45 AM Jay Dugger duggerj@mindspring.com
wrote:
> My copy of _Nanomedicine_ arrived yesterday, and I should have it on
> Monday. (Oregon Health Sciences University bookstore closes over the
> weekend.) Does anyone know of an on-line group devoted to reading and
> discussing the book? Yes, I know if I want one and none exists then I
> have the obligation to start one or keep quiet. None need mention
> this.

Don't think starting one would be all that hard. I once had a space
colonization discussion list -- from 1996 to 1997. The response and traffic
were low, but it was easy to setup and maintain.

I imagine with this more _realistic_ idea -- and I'm all for space
colonization, but sans nanotech, I don't see it happening for at least the
next two or three decades -- would attract more attention. I'd be more in
favor of a list with some rules so the discussion stays on topic and is
productive -- as opposed to it merely be a gossip area or a place for people
to one-up each other.

I have yet to get a copy of _Nanomedicine_ -- mostly because of the price
issue. (And it's probably beyond my understanding right now.:)

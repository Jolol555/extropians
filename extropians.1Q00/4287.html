Hi Extropes,

There is an IAU Colloquium for Solar System Dust taking place
in about a month in Canterbury, England.

This colloquium will be combining several conferences- The
twice-a-decade IAU Colloqium and our annual
"Galileo/Ulyssses/Cassini/Stardust Dust meeting", plus other
special workshops and discussions.

IAU Colloquium 181/COSPAR Colloquium 11
Dust in the solar system and other planetary systems
University of Kent at Canterbury,England. April 10-14 2000
http://www.ukc.ac.uk/physical-sciences/space/

I learned that the Tuesday sesssions, which are devoted to
Galileo/Ulyssses/Cassini/Stardust science will be free, so anyone can
walk in and listen. (However for the other days, you would need to
pay.)

The Session Program and abstracts are on-line now.

Session 1 Meteors
Session 2 Meteoroid Streams
Session 3 Zodiacal light observations
Session 4 Zodiacal dust and dynamics
Session 5 Galileo Ulysses Cassini I
Session 6 Galileo Ulysses Cassini II
Session 7 Special Stardust Session
Session 8 Poster Session Rutherford College
Session 9 Dust in Other Solar Systems
Session 10 Edgeworth-Kuiper Belt
Session 11 Dust Sources
Session 12 Cometary Dust
Session 13 Rosetta
Session 14 Grain aggregation and optical studies
Session 15 Applications and Links
Session 16 Dust in Earth orbit
Session 17 Origins?

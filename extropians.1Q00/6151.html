>
>If I was a little boy in some African village without wires or phone, I'd
>take stale cake in a New York minute.
>

You wouldn't, if that New York minute were purchased at New York
prices or, in the case of Irritatium, several orders of magnitude
over New York prices.

The thing was stupid from the start. I believe that burning in those
birds will be one of the most constructive admonitions we're received
lately from those in whom we entrust our connected futures. It will
teach us, as it once again has me, that there is a big difference
between good ideas and appropriate delivery on them.

Instead, let's encourage appropriate technology which promises to
deliver on the same promises at rates they can afford without further
disabling "help" from the North. It exists. They're already using it.
If you want to make a difference, invest in it.

Your pal,

Barlow

	+-------------------------------------------------------+


	William Fowler
	
	US astrophysicist.  In 1983 he and Subrahmanyan Chandrasckhar 
	were awarded the Nobel Prize for Physics for their work on
	the life cycle of stars and the origin of chemical elements.
	Fowler concentrated on research into the abundance of 
	helium in the universe.  Taking into account the light
	elements, and considering the build-up of heavier elements,
	Fowler along with Hoyle and Wagoner was able to 
	calculate the helium abundance in the universe to 1%.

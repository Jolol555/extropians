This years' Cato Institute/Forbes ASAP Technology & Society Conference will explore education for high-tech, online arbitratrion, spread spectrum, genetically altered plants, domain name competition, and nanotech.

The event will be held from November 4-5 in Milpitas, California (close to San Jose). Speakers will include Bill Schrader of PSINet, Tom Siebel of Siebel Systems, education reformer Tim Draper, and Stan Williams, one of the scientists working on the molecular computer switch.

Visit http://www.cato.org/events/techconf99/index.html (or go to cato.org and click on the Tech&Society icon) for more information and registration.

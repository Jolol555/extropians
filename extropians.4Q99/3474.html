Don't laugh, the nanotube may turn out to be the rod delay line of the future. Phonons going down one of these can have wavelengths that are orders of magnitude shorter than photons. Remember that your hard disk is a set of data tracks that each look like serial delay line memory (while it is spinning). I once owned a G-15 computer that used tracks on a magnet drum as serial delay memory, in which the bits had to be read and restored every rev to get them around a small patch of the surface that was the "fast" memory.

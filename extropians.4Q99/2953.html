It's also worthy of note that all known species of cephs have *really* short lifespans by human lights.
Senescence/death appear to be plugged in to sexual maturity in cephs, more so than even, say, mustelids.

So now, posit the ceph uplift requires extending their lifespan. If that were done to the germline and
it got out into the world's ceph population somehow, what would the impact be? With, or without, changing
their go-for-broke reproduction strategies (e.g., planktonic larvae in some species)?

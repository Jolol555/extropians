I don't doubt that your EV from these trades, and every trade you make, is probably very high. But as a poker player I have to say that regularly putting 5% of your bankroll on a single bet--even if every bet has positive expectation--produces an uncomfotably high probability of ruin. My poker expectation is about 2 bets per hour; but I have upswings and downswings of 100 bets now and then. It's not uncommon for pros to have bad _months_. Things generally level out at the months-to-years level, but you better have the bankroll to outlast those swings. I keep track of my play and can quote you both expectation and variance (19!). Do you have similar stats for your trading?

Hi Bob,

As I see it, the thread moved from a discussion of the need for rigorous science in the study of societal evolution to one of debate concerning the role of ethics in human affairs.

Since I find both these topics interesting, it seemed appropriate to try to keep the threads going.

In my view, extropians/transhumanists need to continually re-emphasize the importance of real science over and above the philosophical musings of social commentators, because social commentary has a habit of tagging along with political agendas and people with axes to grind.

As for ethics, I think science _can_ clarify this miasma if researchers can overcome the politically correct aversion to the study of biology as it relates to human behavior and systems of belief.

On 8/23/01 12:50 PM, "Sabine Atkins" <sabine@posthuman.com> wrote:
>
> I've been a while on the low carb diet, too.
> My experiences weren't that good. In the beginning I lost weight, but
> that was mostly water. Then I reached a plateau were I couldn't loose
> any more weight at all. The low carb diet book recommended drinking
> plenty of water and cutting out even artificially sweetened drinks. I
> found out that low carb diets do not work well when you have
> hypothyroidism (which I have). About the same time my mother-in-law, who
> was also on the low carb diet, told me that her hair began falling out.
> She never had that problem before.

I'm sure it is influenced somewhat by genetics as to what works well and
what doesn't. I happen to have a very stable metabolic system that doesn't
really overreact to anything, so I'm lucky in that respect. My weight loss
wasn't huge (five pounds), but that was counter-balanced by an aggressive
resistance training schedule, so I probably added some muscle mass at the
same time. What *was* obvious is that the couple pockets of noticeable fat
on my body practically evaporated in front of my eyes, ones that had been
stubbornly resistant for years.

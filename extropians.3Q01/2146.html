On Tue, 24 Jul 2001, Mike Lorrey wrote:

> Gee, Gene, why is it that the worldwide attack on intellectual
> property rights in general comes out of the same people who are
> involved in open source? Its endemic to the paradigm.

Um, no. Open source software is the software that ships with source, and a
license allowing you to modify and redistribute it.

I may or may not have an opinion on IP rights, but it is strictly
irrelevant to the issue.

Open source software pays, primarily because you're engaging in a non-zero
sum transaction, where freeloading doesn't bite since the reproduction
costs are carried by the freeloaders.

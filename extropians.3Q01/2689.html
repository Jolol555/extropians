"Michael M. Butler" wrote:
>
> See INESAP Technical Report No. 1, Seventh Edition (Sep 2000). The fourth chapter details seven physical processes which
> could be used to make low-yield nukes *or* non-fission triggers for theater-range nukes:
>
> 1) Subcritical fission-burn

This one isn't "non-fission" by the strictest meaning. But it does only require, e.g., 0.7 g of Pu to get you a 12 t
yield, if you have enough other stuff (e.g., antiprotons) to produce a flood of neutrons. The antiprotons used in this
case are a distinct category from the more general "antimatter" case.

> Al, I stated that whites and non-whites commit about the same number of

I have to take significant exception to this, Barbara. Crime studies
don't only look at arrest and conviction data. Some deal with surveys of
victims for a more victim-centric view of crime as it is committed, not
as it is enforced. For example, the FBI claims that there are only
around 90,000 defensive gun uses per year, yet surveys show that the
actual number is somewhere around 2.5 million per year. (Google up the
work of Gary Kleck) Similarly, victim reports of the race of
perpetrators (and the race of their victims) shows that blacks commit
90% of the crime perpetrated against other blacks, but a majority of the
crime perpetrated against whites.

Of course, this could likely leave out victimless crimes, but other
studies of white collar crime, and its prevalence, should shed some
light on this. I would buy the fact that whites likely commit more white
collar crime than blacks, that being their more likely environment, but
what are the actual numbers?

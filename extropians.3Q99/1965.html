I've been involved somewhat recently with an angel group in Atlanta (angelatlanta.com), so maybe I can comment a bit. I was not at Extro4, so perhaps you got some tips on companies in need of funding. That is the big problem with forming an angel group- finding the companies. By the time you see a real press release from a company, they are probably past the angel stage and into the big VC money (past where we might want to invest). So either you have to get people into the angel group who will be able to consistently find these startups at their early stage, or you will have to advertise your group sufficiently well so that the startups know about you.

The angel site I mention above does provide online biz plans and info for the angels to read, but I think RL meetings with potential investments are required. You have to be able to meet the management team and really question them before you will have enough confidence to invest. Video conferencing _might_ be able to fill this gap.

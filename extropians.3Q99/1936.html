There was a discussion of iqtest.com a few months ago. You could check the archives. We concluded that they were intentionally fudging results up about 15 points, a full standard deviation. Which would still give you an IQ of 137, if that test actually measures IQ in the same way that other IQ tests do. 137 is still quite good.

I don't think iqtest.com is offering a test that would be recognized by most psychologists as a "real" IQ test. The standard, accepted tests have been widely tested over a long period and subject to intense peer review, which I don't think their tests have been.

All that said, I wouldn't take it too seriously. It's how you use it.

Some college degrees can be great for advancing your career, but the statistics show that getting a degree in the humanities won't help much if at all:
http://www.forbes.com/forbes/98/1228/6214104a.htm

As a successful college dropout, I agree that a degree or lack thereof is not proof or disproof of intelligence.

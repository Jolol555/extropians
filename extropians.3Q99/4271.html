I'm also serious, BTW, in helping to pitch stories about cryonics to the media. One of the aspects of media coverage on cryonics has had somewhat reactionary tone--the producers of a show think up their story idea and go find someone who wants to be frozen to say a few words. That person has very little control over how they are portrayed. We could easily elevate the tone of that portrayal by proactively approaching producers with a specific story idea and background information.

I haven't tackled it before, because the LES in my area got a five-page cover story in the City Paper, which is widely read, and they did a good job of convincing the reporter to dig a little deeper. The story was written in a lighthearted and yet informative tone, and it has a wonderful front page shot of then-president, Mark Mugler, lying in the temporary cooling bin. More of that sort of coverage would only help, and I could mail a copy of it to anyone who wants to see it.

Y'know, that may be the pap they're teaching you in school, but get into the real world.

  1. every where I see replanting, when it is on a hillside it is randomized, when in the valley it is regularized.
  2. at the turn of the century, NH was 90% clear cut. Now just 10% is cut and developed. The regrowth everywhere is diverse and random. The areas that were replanted by hand with evergreen trees are the best dense cover for deer and other animals. They take shelter there, and venture out into the hardwoods only to forage for food.

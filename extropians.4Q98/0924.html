>>1) It is not clear that demand can ever be "saturated."
>
> You sit down in an Italian restaurant, a big plate of
>pasta in front of you. You eat, and eat, and eat, and eat.
>When you can eat no more, your demand is saturated.
>Multiply by five billion.
> Every human on the planet--a finite number--is home,
>in a very big house, wandering around thinking,"I should
>close down this wing of the house, I never use it."
>Demand is saturated.

I don't think you appreciate the vast number of dimensions along which demand can increase. Yes, researaunt meal pounds of food has long ago saturated, and yet such meals cost more as people get richer. Why? Because demand has increased along quality and diversity of ingredients, care and technology of preperation, rapidity of retooling to follow food fashions, quality of decor, convenience of location, etc. Total demand for restaraunt food doesn't saturate until *all* dimensions saturate. And total economic demand doesn't saturate until demand for *all* kinds of products and services saturates. Leave one service with one unsaturated dimension, and you aren't there.

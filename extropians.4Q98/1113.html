It comes from Robert A. Heinlein's novel STRANGER IN A STRANGE LAND (a book which inspired the Church of All Worlds, publisher of GREEN EGG Magazine) and was usually used in that book as part of the phrase "grok it in its fullness." This meant to understand something completely, from the internal (and all external) perspectives. Of course, this is an existential and phenomenological impossibility in practice, but it remains an abstract ideal by which it is useful to measure/compare one's mental grasp of something, and attempt to asymptotically attain.

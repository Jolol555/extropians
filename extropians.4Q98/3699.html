> with and Attitudes Toward Cryonic Preservation

This is a great paper that makes interesting reading as a probe of the effect of various demographic factors on attitudes about the future and technological advance in general. One thing it does bear out is my experience that most people significantly overestimate the cost of cryonic suspension: I often hear people guess that it must cost half a million dollars or some such figure, when $50k, financed easily by life insurance (especially if you're young and healthy), is the right number for a neurosuspension.

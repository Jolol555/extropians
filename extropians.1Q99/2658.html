Jonathan, I once managed to accidentally make my dog superstitious and even
perform a worship service of me. I always gave him only dry dog food, and not
too much of even that. One day, just for the hell of it, I cooked two steaks and

in the grass on his back. For several days after, whenever I came out of the
house, he would immediately roll on his back and start worshipping. {8^D
Oh mighty human, accept my humble canine supplication...

He was a good dog. Shoulda had him frozen.

> Religion is a primitive form of science - attempting to understand and

Perhaps you are right. I can imagine a group of cave humans, superstitious and
afraid of everything, for good reason. One day when there was a wicked thunderstorm, as they cowered in the back of the cave, one gutsy guy stood
in the opening. Whenever a big flash happened, he would spread his arms wide,
and right away a boom! After that, noooo one messed with that guy. Power!
Had all the ladies he wanted, many offspring. And no wonder he wanted to
propagate the god gene. He was in tight with god!

When you think it over, it really is no wonder that every human society has
the god(s) meme. There are plenty of mechanisms that would start and propagate it, whereas those mechanisms that overturn it are few and sophisticated, such as logic and reason, forces which in human society are
fragile indeed. spike

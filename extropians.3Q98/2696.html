It has been easier on the other countries recently because we (1) have been willing to give them the technology necessary to process and manucacture goods out of their own resources, (2) have allowed them to retain those resources in order to process them themselves, and (3) have refrained from destroying either resource extraction facilities or the factories to process and manufacture goods from them. Our willingness to permit all this has a lot to do with our controlling financial interest in the extraction and processing of resources into manufactured goods, in tandem with the exploitation of cheap local labor (the human resource), which entails that an even further enhanced lion's share of the profit realized from such endeavors still flows into the coffers of multinational corporations based in the industrialized countries.

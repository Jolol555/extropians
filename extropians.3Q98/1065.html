> Won't extended life worsen overpopulation problems?

  • 1962. Man-made radiation and man-made chemicals, particularly pesticides, are destroying the biosphere, poisoning the food chain, and threatening to wipe out man in 20-odd years(Carson) .* 1968. Because of overpopulation, hundreds of millions will die of starvation in the '70s (Ehrlich)
  • 1969. "Our present system of technology is not merely consuming this capital (land, water and other resources), but threatening - probably within the next 50 years- to destroy it irreparably" . Solution: impose a moratorium on technological innovation (Commoner).
  • 1972. The Earth is running out of resources (eg. oil by 1992) (Club of Rome) . * 1975. Nuclear weapons, energy and waste, oceanic oil residues, ozone destruction, the automobile, industrial accidents, lung diseases, cancer, overpopulation and famine will make it difficult for the human race to "get itself much past the year 2000" (Wald) .
  • 1976. Man-made aerosol emissions will cause a new ice-age (Ponte) .
  • 1976/77. Biotechnology (eg. genetic engineering) will cause plagues that will ravage the Earth (Cavalieri) .
  • 1982. Acid rain is causing Waldsterben, the death of forests and lakes in North America and Europe. Ten-year German and American studies refuted this theory.
  • 1983. Nuclear winter will end the human species . Therefore the west must unilaterally disarm - NOW!
  • 1984. The UN Environment Program declared 25% of the world's land area to be threatened by desertification. However, latest data show no long term increase in global desert area.
  • 1988. Increased greenhouse gases will cause catastrophic global warming (Ehrlich et al) .
  • 1989. Humanity has less than 40 years left on Earth unless a Global Emergency is declared and energy consumption cut 70-80%.
  • 1991. If Saddam Hussein torches Kuwait's oil wells they may take 7 years to put out , smoke could lower temperatures over a quarter of the Earth's surface , causing droughts in India and Bangladesh and "massive agricultural failures in the US" (Sagan) .
  • 1992. The "man-made" ozone hole will allow increased ultra-violet radiation to wither crops, destroy ecosystems and cause skin cancer . Therefore the developing world must be denied the cheap refrigeration we enjoy.
  • 1994. The Earth is cooling down - again! Culprit: man-made aerosols - again! (Pearce)
